Dear Theo,

It is 6:27am in Vancouver right now. Two short months ago your mother and I were standing at a bus stop at the corner of Wesbrook Mall and W. 16th ave. as large wet snowflakes drifted down from the dark morning sky. It was around this time that my frustration and annoyance with metro Vancouver's public transit network was peaking, having been waiting for about 40 minutes by that time. And you were still 2 hours away from making your appearance.

As you complete month two, you have already managed to accumulate residences in three different apartments and have touched down in three countries. This puts you slightly ahead of your brother and sister's schedule - it took them well into their second month to achieve those same stats. And perhaps this is a harbinger of things to come. The ago old push of the younger sibling to catch up with the older. Often leading to the tension in which you may be surpassing them in relative terms but coming up short in the absolute.

But what do you care for this at this point? How sweetly oblivious you are to these concepts, and to whether you are sitting on a couch in a Vancouver basement or a small French apartment? Or on an airplane for that matter.

You awareness up to this point focussed almost exclusively on the internal. Hunger. Fatigue. Warmth. The discomfort of built up gas. The strain of pooping. But, like a seed that germinates and starts life below the soil you too are starting to send up your very first shoots into the open air. Yesterday while I held you you matched my gaze and smiled. And not just a fart smile but a smile of connection. 

This process of connection is just beginning but one that will shape your life. Like a spiders web that entraps and links people, ideas, experiences and desires, all with you at the center.
