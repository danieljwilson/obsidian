
Dear Theo,

You are 3 years old. Three spirals around the sun.

Three years ago early on a snowy Vancouver morning we first met you, but it has been a loverly process of three years over which I have really gotten to know you. And that knowing has sharply accelerated over the past four months as you have made what feels like a feels like an abrupt transition from baby to person. A large part of that has been your talking. I remember in July lying on the couch next to you and having you repeat words after I said them. You could do it, but had a very limited spontaneous vocabulary. I clearly recall the night when we put you down to bed in your pack and play and instead of dropping directly off to sleep as you, much to our delight, had done almost every evening for the first two and a half years of your life, you stayed awake for an extended period of time just saying, and at times yelling, your name, over and over. Theo. Theo. Theo. Theo!

Now it is you, Theo, who serves as our morning sentinel; calling out with an excited urgency as soon as the daily 7am church bells start to chime (the signal that the kids are allowed to get up) "The bells! The bells! Papa, I hear the bells!". It was only a few weeks ago that you used to charmingly say "I see the bells!", not quite nailing the correct verb.

These of course are the same bells that rang, unnoticed, on your first birthday, when you were sleeping in the same bedroom, but with your brother Amos in the bed that you now claim. Echos. And you are also now in the same classroom that Amos was in two years ago, with the same teacher. It seems both completely normal and somehow impossible - this underlining and circling of the passage of time, that is so easy to ignore at our age. And while I miss the one year old Theo, I love three year old Theo so much. And the level of joy and laughter we experience with three year old Theo is definitely a welcome contrast - I'm sure for both us - to uncomfortable and extremely constipated 1 year old Theo. I  get reminded of this time whenever I find myself in the water section of the grocery store and see the Hépar brand water which used to be the only water you would get in your bottles in the vain hopes that the claims that it helped relieve constipation might prove true. 

I’m a decade you will be a teen. 

When I told your Nounou Patricia it was your third birthday she immediately responded that, as a parent, this was the best age.

I think I have to agree. At least for now. There are so many things I appreciate and love about you at this age.

I love your laughter.

I love and fear your desire to jump off of things.

I love how when we arrive at the beach you go immediately to work turning rocks and twigs into trains and totally ignoring the outside world for a half hour straight. 

I love how you love to bury yourself under the comforter at the bottom of the bed pretending it is your cave.

I love when I see you playing with your brother and sister.

I loved when we finished the Tour de Mont Blanc hike and we were walking into Courmayeur and I told you that "hi" was "ciao" in Italy and you started saying "ciao" to everyone we passed.

I love your sense of honesty. Recently, when I was reprimanding you and I asked you if you were going to throw things in the house again you tearfully said “yes”. Not defiantly, but just genuinely and honestly, and even with a touch of regret.

I love your sense of humor. Walking around the apartment with a box over your head.  

I love witnessing this transition from baby to person. 

Though a transition suggests a change. Perhaps it is more accurate to think of it as a reveal. And may the revelations continue in this third year of life.

Curious about the next year for both you and us.
