Theo, Two months old

  

Theo, 

  

There you are, two months old. I saw you this afternoon, as people do these days, on FaceTime— that means on the screen of my computer. To you that will be just the way people communicate, to me it’s still a little amazing, because it’s fairly new in my lifetime.

  

When your dad went to France for a year of school when he was 18, the way we communicated was writing letters (I still have a file with his letters in them). Even phone calls were rare, because they were expensive in those days.

  

But really, that’s not what I’m here to talk about. It’s you. When I was watching you today I was amazed. You seem so very grown up, different from the brand new baby you were when I last saw you. Your neck is strong and your face is changing and, of course, you’re beautiful. It’s like you were noticing, paying attention to what was going on. 

  

Your dad said you smiled at him yesterday. You have no idea how exciting that is for the grown ups around you. Baby smiles are like kindling on a fire, making our love burn ever brighter. 

  

Right now it’s frozen winter here in Caribou River. When I look out the window I see white, shining and shadowed on the ground, and I know, despite the sun, it is very cold out there. You would need to wear all your snowsuits, one on top of the other. 

  

Where you are, where you’ve just arrived, it’s a warmer season, and a whole new country. I wonder if you notice the new language you’re hearing, from your nanny, Yasmine, or when you’re out and about in the town. Or, if it isn’t the words but the tone that you notice now.

  

I’m thinking a lot about when I’ll see you next; almost in time for your next birthday, and how much you’ll have changed again before that. About how much I’m looking forward to seeing you, to holding you, and, seeing you smile.

  

Love,

Nana
