# 18 Month Birthday
#writing 

Dear Amos and Rilka,

It is two weeks before the big day. I am sitting in what has been provisionally dubbed my "office" next to a giant industrial fan, that is aimed at my bike, which is set up on the Wahoo Kickr Core trainer I bought on the drive over to Montreal. The bike faces the window, out which can be seen the grey and darkening sky of a 3:54 Montreal winter afternoon, through the branches of the large branchy but leafless tree that grows directly in front of the house.

We have spend most of the month in some state of illness. Fevers, coughing, stomach aches, head aches, vomiting, diarrhea with constantly running noses and the sculptural mucous-based under-nose lunar landscapes they create. After an initial synchronized illness you have now entered more of a relay-type situation, where, currently, Rilka is the unfortunate baton bearer. A 39+ degree fever has meant a day largely spent lying on either my or your mom's chest, thumb in mouth, eyes half closed. This is in stark contrast to your brother, Amos, who received the Christmas gift of a return to health (ignoring the running nose), and has re-committed to days of high speed crawling and high volume laughter.

It has been just over 5 months since my last (delayed) birthday letter, and seeing the gap in dates from 1 year to 18 months was a bit startling after looking at the preceding monthly entries from your first year. 

In some respects things have not changed all that dramatically since your year birthday. Rilka is still walking. Amos is still crawling. No one is talking. Everyone is pooping their pants. But for your parents this six month time period has been one of the most hectic in recent memory. One might have thought that the previous year's scenario of travel to a new continent with newborn twins might be hard to beat, however, in many ways 0-6 months was a delightful cakewalk compared to 12-18. That was entirely our doing though, not yours.

A brief summary of your whereabouts during the six month time period includes:
- Two weeks in Burlington, visiting Donna's parents
- One month in Nova Scotia (of which 2 sweltering weeks were spent in quarantine) visiting my mother
- Three months in Toronto
- One and a half months in Montreal

A brief summary of your parents doings during this time period includes:
- Your Dad attended a 10hr/day 3 week neuroscience summer school while your mother looked after you mostly on her own
- We moved all of our belongings out of our previous house at 95 Follis and into a storage facility on a sweltering afternoon, directly before...
- Traveling to lake Skootamata for a weekend with the family and relatives of Donna's mother
- We moved to Nova Scotia for a month
- We found and rented a condo in Toronto
- We searched for, and bid on an apartment in Montreal
- We tried to figure out daycare option for Toronto given that we had been told we were currently ineligible for subsidy support
- We got pre-approved on a mortgage
- We managed to finagle a daycare subsidy in Toronto after all
- We found out that we were outbid on the apartment in Montreal
- We moved our family back to Toronto
- We moved our belongings out of storage and into our rented condo
- Donna starting full time work with SSG
- Daniel starting working full time on his PhD
- You both started going to the Breadalbane YMCA daycare - much to your chagrin initially
- Donna completed her final paper
- Daniel TA'd a course
- Donna TA'd a course
- We furnishing our apartment in Toronto
- We found out we could get the house in Montreal after all
- We figured out financing for the house in Montreal and then re-figured it out when we were told that we would need to more than double our anticipated down payment
- Donna completed her PhD
- Daniel dealt with completing the end of cycle off-boarding for minutiae as well as the new Android version
- We all traveled to Montreal to do the inspection of the apartment
- Your Dad then went back to Montreal to completely furnish the apartment over the course of 48 hours
- We tried to find renters for our apartment in Montreal but then decided to actually move to Montreal in December
- We tried to find renters for our place in Toronto for December
- We moved to Montreal
- You started a new daycare in Montreal
- We worked to finish furnishing our apartment in Montreal
- We had our Montreal roommate move out which meant we had to find a new Montreal roommate (who just today told us he is moving out)
- We all took turns being constantly sick for a month beginning in early December
- Daniel mostly finished up the Data Science for Social Good volunteer project he had been working on over the past 4 months
- We canceled our Christmas plants 24 hours before departure and did a Montreal Christmas
- We found out our Toronto tenants are moving out and so are back looking for new tenants for our Toronto apartment
- We took you for your 4th COVID tests since someone at your daycare got COVID and the garderie was shuttered for 2 weeks

And now, today, with your grandmother, we are celebrating your 18 month birthday. 

I feel like this past six months for both of us has been a setting in place of systems. Of building capacity. Such that we can really bloom over the course of this next 6 months and year. 

I am really and truly looking forward to this on both of our behalf's.

Love,
Dad
