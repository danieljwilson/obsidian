Dear Sandy,

I received the news about Murrin's passing this past week. My mom had kept me abreast of her health issues, but somehow it all felt unbelievable and then this latest news felt most unbelievable of all. Unbelievable not in the sense that these things don't happen, but in the sense that I simply didn't want to believe it.

Talking with my mom she asked me about my memories of Murrin. As I thought back I realized that my memories of Murrin mainly took the form of a general feeling, rather than a series of specific incidents. Of course there are specifics. Like recollections of playing charades in the living room with the Christmas tree up, a visit by you and Murrin brightening the slightly dreary post-Christmas hangover. Murrin's laugh. Bright and smiling. Murrin's amused groan, generally in response to one of your jokes, which, after delivering, you had laughed at loudest of anyone. And then everyone's laughter at Murrin's reaction.

But it is the feeling that stands out for me. A feeling of warmth and kindness. It was funny that my parent's friends who did not have kids of their own were, in fact, the friends I most enjoyed interacting with. It always felt so easy and comfortable spending time with you and Murrin. When it was announced that you were coming for a visit, it was like hearing that the next day's weather forecast had been updated to something a bit better than originally anticipated.  

My feeling, my love, for Murrin felt to me largely a reflection of the goodness that poured out of her, as opposed to something self-generated. She was a lovely person. 

I hope that we can cross paths one of these days Sandy - I would love to see you and catch up and introduce my kids to you. 

Love,

Daniel
