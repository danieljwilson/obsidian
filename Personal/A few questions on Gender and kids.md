[[Writing]]

Last night my mother let me know that Rose was planning to arrive bearing gifts of leotards and dresses for Rilka.

Upon hearing the news I could feel numerous emotions starting to swirl in my head. Gratitude for Rose's thoughtfulness and care for Rilka. Bewilderment about the role of dress in gender. Confusion as to why my sister would not check in with me about giving my child gendered clothes.

And mainly a larger question that has been nagging me for years, but much more intensely since becoming the father of a boy and girl twin - why do we even have these intense gender separations at such early stages? And why are they so stubbornly persistent even in this so-called modern age and not least among those who might consider themselves the most enlightened? And more importantly what are the downstream effects of this societally imposed dichotomy? 

To start with the first question, why does this division - and specifically I mean the pre-puberty division of boys and girls - exist in the first place? I assume it served some function at some point in time. Perhaps in our hunter/gatherer days, which are really not so long ago, one can reasonably imagine that the separation of gender into gendered roles from a young age was based on the future physiological changes that would determine the "career" of that child - and really they would still be children when they started that career. Specifically hunters and mother/gatherers. 

Why do parents tolerate, and even implicitly and explicitly encourage, starting as toddlers, a positive outlook on same gender kids 

Why is a princess, something that has historically been predicated on having two X chromosomes, something that we even introduce to our children, especially as some sort of ideal? 

Why do people pick, just as two archetypes, sleeping beauties over Lyras? I grant that it is hard to avoid - that it is tricky when you live in a society that seems to have auto-piloted (well with plenty of encouraging nudges from the toy-industrial complex) itself into this replication of norms.

But what happened to "be the change"?
Is it mean to enact that change through our kids? Or is it in the end serving them and just making our lives more difficult in the meantime? Are parents just so tired they can't bear to swim against what feels like an overwhelming tide? I have definitely got that sense from a number of parents I have spoken with.

Is it just a phase?

And if it is does it leave anything behind?

And if it is something they grow out of, like a bad habit, then do we not feel guilty for giving them this bad habit in the first place?

And perhaps most confounding, to me, and which has turned these feelings, intuitions, and thoughts into questions, rather than assertions, is why does my partner, an ex-Olympic athlete with a PhD and my sister, a lesbian artist and self-proclaimed feminist, disagree with me so strongly when I protest the giving of frilly dresses and sequined semi-high heeled shoes to my 3 year old daughter?

And to not have this just be a list of grievances/complaints, 

Why not have identities that are inherently mutable?
- explorer
- puzzler
- acrobat

Or 
- hufflepuff
- slytherin 
- griffendor

Or just anything that is based on personality and interests rather than the presence of a Y chromosome?
