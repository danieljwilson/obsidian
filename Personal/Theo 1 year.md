The bells at the church next to me are ringing non-stop at the moment. I am sure their ringing must coincide with the moment parents and child, somewhere in the world, are laying eyes on each other for the very first time. Ringing out those first seconds of seeing, of noticing, of trying to take it all in, however impossible that may be. And they, as you were, pushed, prodded, coaxed, and suddenly yanked into a world of light and air. I wonder where in the world the bells were ringing when you were born? Born. So much wonder contained in a single syllable. I find I have to remind myself at times that just because remarkable things can happen more than once shouldn't make them any less remarkable. It can be easy to confuse rare and remarkable.

The ringing of church bells used to be how people would adjust their clocks and watches in the early days of personal time pieces - those both tending to lose or gain time at a rapid clip. Luckily, no one ahas been setting their clocks by my monthly letters to you, as those clocks would have been falling farther and farther out of sync since your 8 month birthday on August 6th. Paradoxically, part of the reason I have not written anything is because there has been so much to write about. The doing has elbowed the writing out of the way and been making a big fuss and lots of noise while the writing has patiently stood waiting for me to remember to call on it. Perhaps I need to make the writing part of the doing.

In any case lets quickly go over a few of the key doings from the past 4 months:

- we had our first camping trip
- Amos and Rilka started a brand new daycare
- your mom decided to change jobs
- your mom interviewed for and got an amazing new job offer
- she decided to take it and we decided to leave Vancouver
- your dad launched his major thesis experiment
- we sold all of our furniture and moved out of unit 207 at 3428 Wesbrook Mall, saying adios after a year in Vancouver
- you mom moved to Montreal with Rilka
- you mom started her new job
- your sister started a new daycare in Montreal
- your dad moved to Nice, along with you and Amos
- you started going to daycare for the first time, a creche, as they are called in France
- your brother started at a new daycare/maternelle in Nice

And now here I am, walking through the streets of Nice on a rainy Sunday afternoon. As I walk I find myself thinking about how normal it seems right now that I consider myself to be in France. Yet if it were 1850 even though the streets and buildings would look very similar and the geographic location would be identical, I would find myself in Italy. Sometimes the world moves under your feet. You are still our baby, but no longer the baby you were. You are a biological ship of Theseus. Yet as we commemorate this change and this passage of time we are also commemorating a return to the start. The completion of a giant circle just shy of 150 million km in circumference. December 6th 2021. December 6th 2022. We are back to the beginning of the literal circle of life. 

New beginnings. That is in many ways the story of our lives. Each day, each encounter is a new beginning. A constant starting over, but with each start happening from a different place, however slight that difference might be. And the hope is that this difference is one of forward movement, of improvement, of discovery, of learning. I am excited to experience all of these differences with you as we join you while you set off on your life's second orbit. I'll be orbiting right alongside you.

Love,
Dad
