### PHASE 1: Draft
-   2 POINTS: Drafts that are 750 words or longer AND have at least one scholarly source plus the textbook cited in the text and in a reference list will receive the full 2 points for this section.

-   1 POINT: Drafts that are 600-750 words AND have at least one scholarly source plus the textbook cited in the text and in a reference list will receive 1 point for this section.
   
-   0 POINTS: Drafts that are under 600 words OR are in bullet-point form OR are missing a scholarly source or the textbook citation will receive 0 points for this section. Drafts missing a reference list OR missing in-text citations will also receive 0 points for this section. Drafts that do not make a serious attempt to complete the assignment may also receive 0 points for this section.

### PHASE 2: Feedback
Your grade for this section will be based only on your **constructive feedback**.
- 1 point for each peer evaluation if your constructive **feedback is specific** and if it **relates to the assignment description** found on Quercus and/or this rubric. Your **feedback must be actionable** (in other words, it ==must ask the peer to DO something== to change their work). You should be sure to **describe specific ways** that the peer could better address that description and improve their work.

You are welcome and encouraged to make mechanical corrections (e.g., grammar, spelling, formatting) to your peer’s paper or to suggest that the peer use more sources, but these ==mechanical corrections do NOT count as constructive feedback==.

All peer drafts, regardless of how well they are completed, could use some peer feedback. It is ==not acceptable to write that a draft is ‘perfect’ and there is nothing to change==.

### PHASE 3: Revise and Reflect
#### Reflection
You must **describe in specific terms how you chose to address the feedback** that your peers gave you. 
==For each piece of constructive feedback== that you received, you should:
-   **Describe how you incorporated that feedback** into your paper, using specific examples from your work.  
OR   
-   **Explain why you chose not to incorporate that feedback** into your paper, justifying your decision. 
Because you will have received 4 peer reviews, you will receive **0.5 points for each review that you specifically reflect upon in your statement.**
