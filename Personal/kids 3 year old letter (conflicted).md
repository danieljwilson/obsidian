Dear Amos and Rilka,

Is it possible that I haven't written a letter


Three. 
Years. 
Old.

If you mapped 21 years to one week then you would have just completed your first full day. So where do we stand after this first day?

Attempting to distill personality from a child's earliest uncoordinated actions and reactions is a harmless and natural pastime of parents, I'm sure, the world over. It is not an entirely fruitless endeavor, though I do believe that we have a tendency to remember the prognostications we made which turned out to be correct much more readily than those that did not.

At three, however, there is much less need for prognosticating.  Now you are truly becoming and embodying a self, a rich individuality with its unique tapestry of quirks and funny bones and definite likes and and even more definite dislikes. 

Rilka, you, for example love all things purple and have demonstrated an equal but opposite passion for bugs. Amos, you delight in watching planes landing and taking off, eating blueberry muffins and hate having to give up your roller bag pulling duties, as recently observed boarding the plane we are currently traveling on.

For Rilka, in particular, since Amos has not, as of yet, deigned to grace us with his, I am sure, witty ripostes, we now have another window into the machinations of your three year old mind. Yourself, you have not only chosen to talk, but have also determined that you will talk in great volume, in both senses of the word. 

Your pronunciation can be a bit of a crapshoot - we will never forget the "shwacka shway-ya" meltdown in St. Paul de Vence (you wanted the tractor trailer) and you charmingly call boogers 'burgers', and say 'I yike' instead of I "like" - but the complexity of your sentences and the thoughts they are attempting to convey is ramping up exponentially. You are also asking a lot of questions. While we are attempting to understand you, you are attempting to understand an entire world. This involves, perhaps unsurprisingly, a lot of "why" queries as you start to build your own causal mental models, trying to figure out the mysterious steps that link up cause and effect. To be honest, often these devolve into tedious stalling tactics but they also occasionally make me stop and pause, like the other day when you asked me, genuinely curious, "Why did you tell Amos to stop crying?" You have, also, it must be said, started to realize that you can use language to mislead. Just yesterday when we asked you if you had broken the hand soap container you were very quick to claim innocence and indite doudou.

Amos, for your part, we have seen a side of you emerge that is joker, performer, and teaser. Today in the airport lounge you noticed a group of girls between the ages of 5 and 10 and went up to the youngest and started pulling some pretty funny faces and physical routines. They loved it. You have basically become a sort of three year old comic mime. We also have a 4 minute video of your, frankly quite amazing, interpretive dance to a Nice street performer's rendition of House of the Rising Sun. You also love to tease Rilka. The enjoyment is often not mutual, but it is hard to miss the delight and wholesome enthusiasm with which you imbue your harassment.

But, behind the teasing and roller bag disagreements there is a clear, deep, and frankly moving sense of empathy and care that you exhibit for each other. On more than one occasion when Rilka in a fit of pique has decided to no longer walk with us and we continue on Amos will. after about a dozen meters, simply stop in his tracks and also refuse to go on - pointing to Rilka with a look of genuine concern. Rilka is also tuned into Amos' sad moments and when she was recently involved in one while we were in the Vancouver airport lounge and Amos was crying because he had just agreed that it was only fair that Rilka had a turn pulling the carry on luggage Rilka turned to Amos and gave him half a baby pickle to soften the blow. Amazingly, it seemed to work.

While I have spent most of this letter talking about details of your emerging personalities I also want to step back for a moment and just think about the fact that you have been in the world and in our lives for over a thousand days. Three years is a significant chunk. In another three years Theo will be walking and talking and you will have just finished your first year of school. You will be well into the *remembered* portion of your life.
