[[letter on kids]]

Dear Rilka and Amos,

Four.
Years.
Old.

I find myself repeating these words to try to make sense of them, to have them sink in. 

Part of the challenge is just the larger human challenge of understanding time. And the personal challenge of appreciating the flow of your own time and your position in that flow.

I still can't believe when someone asks my age and I have to say '45'. When and how did that happen? My decades digit is equal to your years digit, and even my years digit is bigger than your years digit. 

In additional attempts to frame your time here on earth - and time in general - I realized that when 4 more years have passed we will (hopefully) be celebrating your birthday with an 80 year old Nana. I find the magnitude of that number striking. I remember when our family friend Fred MacInnes turned 80 years ago and I was stunned to consider that he could possibly be 80 years old. Before that I hadn't really thought of people "becoming" 80, I just thought of people, in particular grandparents, as BEING 80, as if they had been magic-ed into existence at that age.

But we are all magic-ed into existence at the same age, of course. Just at different points in time. The fact that the Nana-magic happened 76 years ago, however, underscores the importance of these next 4 years - pre your respective 8 and 80 year birthdays - in terms of maximizing Nana's adventures with you. 

One particularly neat thing about these impending adventures is the fact that these will be some of your very first best memories. Though maybe you have already had your first memory. Maybe it was today at the pool with Nana and Aunt Cat. Or a few days earlier when meeting a very enthusiastic Leo for the first time.

Speaking of best, even though this may be another birthday you don't remember, I am pretty confident that it has been the best one you have yet experienced. Admittedly we did set the bar low, initially, with birthday 1 being celebrated alone in a Toronto basement Airbnb that promised a pool but delivered a dirt patch while under COVID lockdown. Compare that to this year when we are invading Aunt Cat's spacious 430 Cherrier apartment with Nana and Leo and an array of streamers and balloons and an ice cream cake (with a weird payment history) and a day spent at the biosphere and the parc jean drapeau swimming pool with divers in the background and a trip to the water park on Mont Royal Avenue. And so many thoughtful presents. And, again, an ice cream cake. You biked back from the party with so many balloons tied to your bike that it was hard to see you and hard to believe that it was still possible to pedal. People stopped and watched and smiled. And then you crashed. A dramatic crash with the popping of a half dozen balloons - but you were completely unfazed, and popped up yourself explaining that now that you were 4 you didn't cry anymore. 

I love this sense of possibility that you embody. That because a day is a birthday that things have changed - that you have changed. Never to cry again.

But while this probably will not be the case, the general idea is in fact true. You are completely full of possibility and change. I feel torn about this, as while I completely love seeing these changes I also understand what these changes mean. That you are growing up. That time is passing. When I look back at photos of you from your first year of life I feel a pang of loss. I will never get to spend more time with that version of you. But of course the benefits outweigh the costs. Each year, each day, I get to know who you are a bit better. I get to see you discover the world and try and build your own mental models of "how things work"- however shaky those models may be.

I think that this ambivalence - the love of seeing you develop and the sadness of losing the previous versions of you - makes me appreciate your growth all the more. I will be sad when Rilka stops saying bargage instead of garbage, not because I have any special fondness for the former term, but just because it means that you are that much incrementally closer to being an adult. And this is even more intense for Amos. I so so badly want you to be able to express your thoughts, your feelings, your **self**, with words. But I also know that this means that you will not be my 4 year old boy of today any more. So while I am sad to lose your previous versions, I am so so excited to meet the future versions of you. At 4 years of age I love who you are in this instant, and am so looking forward to all the instants that lie between now and your next birthday celebration.

Love,
Dad
