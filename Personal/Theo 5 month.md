Dear Theo,

  

You are back to where you started. 3428 Wesbrook Mall. I guess technically it started at 4500 Oak St. at the BC Women's hospital, or, extra-technically, at 3491 Avenue de Hotel-de-Ville in Montreal. Wherever you wish to position the start, however, here we are now at our apartment in Larkspur House - which I had no idea, until this very moment was a genus of flowering plant comprising about 300 species of annuals and perennials. According to Google the most commonly asked question about larkspur is "How poisonous is larkspur?". The answer includes the bolded text that "all parts of all larkspur species are poisonous" and goes on to describe the neuro-muscular paralysis caused by this flower which leads to respiratory failure, bloat and, often, death. They also point out that there is no known treatment. All which makes me kind of hope that our building name was just the whimsy of some overworked copywriter sitting at a desk in their open plan office, and not actually related to the flora surrounding our us.

  

That said, perhaps we **are** indeed surrounded by larkspur, but your close proximity from such an early age will serve to slowly inoculate you to its effects. A bit like Westley (aka the Dread Pirate Roberts) and his iocane powder immunity in the Princess Bride. So you could find yourself well positioned to defeat any future Vizzinis you may run into. 

  

I have to imagine it is common for parents to wonder about their kids' future abilities. Where will they excel? Which poisons will they be impervious to? It is a common interview question, at least in silicon valley, to ask of applicants what their "superpower" is. This may perhaps be a reflection of the fact that silicon valley seems to be largely run by men-children who spend a lot of time watching Marvel movies, but I still find it is interesting to think about where your future relative strengths lie. For the moment it seems like they are fairly literal. i.e. you are quite, and surprisingly, strong. This was initially noted by the delivery doctors and nurses and has continued to remain the case. You have been able to stand up and support your own wait from early days and are just getting better and better at it. We joke that you may start walking before crawling and it is a joke but it is not entirely a joke. 

  

As a parent I am particularly interested in where and how you will exceed me. I mainly envision this as the product of your interior alchemy of interests and abilities, but there is another component as well to consider - which is just that of time. When I imagine you in the future I have a tendency to project myself as I am now, but clearly that is ignoring the inevitable physical processes of time. There will be a scant 3 weeks separating your ability to go to a bar in the Unites States and my ability to benefit from senior citizen discounts. Just the writing of that ineluctable fact I experience as an intensely dissonant experience. It evokes the jarring sensation of time collapsing. But there is also a sort of aesthetically pleasing symmetry to the different ways in which time and aging will lead to frequent intersections as we trend in opposite directions. The day when we both can run at the same speed. The day when you spend an equal amount of time on social media as me. The day when we share the same amount of knowledge about current music. The day we eat the same number of pancakes for breakfast.

  

Some of these points of convergence will be noteworthy. The first time you win a game of horse where I am actually trying, for example. Others will pass by unremarked. But they are coming. And I'm sure it will start sooner than I expect. 

  

Love,

Dad
