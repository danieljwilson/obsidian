Dear Theo,

One half of your first year of life, down the drain. You are currently attached to my chest looking at my three computer monitors and enthusiastically making raspberries as I type this letter. Actually now the raspberries have morphed into babbling, a series of serviceable bla-bla-da-das. Now the arms have become involved. Flapping up and down with your left hand while grabbing at my sweatshirted forearm with your right. Gone are the days when we would sit you in your reclining chair and your mother and I would just kind of forget about you for a couple of hours as you watched the world go by, wide-eyed.

A few days ago your mother called to me from the bedroom and when I went over she pointed at a small creature standing up by the bedside table leafing through a magazine. That was you. And it was already fairly impressive, but then I learned that you had actually been left lying on the middle of the bed encircled by a pillow barrier when your mother had left the room - and what I was now seeing is what she had returned to. Oh how I would love to have video of that intervening process. Some people, you may find, are a bit uptight about surveillance and privacy these days, but I want more of it! 

Thinking about privacy leads me to consider the current zeitgeist-y topics and to wonder about where we will be, as a society, on those topics in the future - or, perhaps more likely, what the new topics will be. What will the world be thinking about privacy by the time you are ready to have conversations on the subject? Will we still be talking about AI and crypto and climate change and Elon Musk and Vladimir Putin and transgender politics, and voter fraud and gasoline prices? 

I think of the world we live in as your world, but in fact you will live in a different world, and in that different world will be a different dad. I expect the me-in-five-years to have a number of resemblances to the current model, but there will inevitably be some differences as well as I pass through the world and accumulate experiences, and knowledge and develop and change.
