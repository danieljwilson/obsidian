The 4th month birthday. 

Since Rilka and Amos interrupted our pizza party to announce their impending arrival in July 2019 we have spent, in addition to a number of shorter time periods, 4 full month long segments of time with you. 

Two in caribou river, in September of 2019 and then in August of 2020, one in Vancouver in December of 2021, and, most recently, and still ongoing, a month in St. Jeannet France, with a final hurrah in Paris.

This is by far the most time we have spent with any other human during this time period. It is, what statisticians might refer to as an outlier. Typically when analyzing data we are encouraged to throw away our outliers so that we don’t distort the information provided by the rest of our data points. However this tendency, in its quest to uncover general principles, can risk tossing out the most interesting occurrences due to the very uniqueness.

You, my dear mother, are highly unique. You come from, I think it is fair to say, a fairly conventional, if not conservative, family and then find yourself living in rural Nova Scotia following an elopement in Terra del Fuego that was preceded by multiple encounters with a person going by the name of “High Rider Rick”. You are notably kind and thoughtful and smart and curious. You are willing to learn and listen and consider, even after three quarters of a century on this planet. You are, yourself, an outlier. But a wonderful generous outlier, an outlier in whose company we look forward to spending many more beautiful chunks of time engaging in a wide variety of outliery activities.
