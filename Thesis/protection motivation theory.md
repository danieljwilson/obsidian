# Notes
Proposes that people protect themselves based on four factors: the perceived severity of a threatening event, the perceived probability of the occurrence, or vulnerability, the efficacy of the recommended preventive behavior, and the perceived [[self-efficacy]].

- theory that was originally created to help clarify fear appraisals
- motivation stems from both the threat appraisal and the coping appraisal
- explains why people engage in unhealthy practices and offers suggestions for changing those behaviors

![Image result for protection motivation theory](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/316278315/figure/fig1/AS:486284008398848@1492950550193/Conceptual-framework-modified-from-protection-motivation-theory-of-Rogers-6.png)

-  Has weaker predictive validity than the [[theory of planned behavior]] (Milne et al., 2000)
