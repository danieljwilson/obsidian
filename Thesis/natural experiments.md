# Notes
Depends on looking for something happening in the world that randomly (or nearly randomly) assigns the treatment to some people and not others.

- ==random (or as if random) variation + always-on data = natural experiment==
- allows for causal estimates from [[non-experimental data]]
- Two different strategies for finding natural experiments:
	1. **start with an always-on data source** and look for random events in the world
	2. **start with a random event in the world** and look for data sources that capture its impact
- the problem is that "nature" does not always randomly deliver the treatment you want to the population of interest...
- you can also look at cases where a secondary treatment encourages people to receive the primary treatment of interest^[This was the case with the draft...where that is strongly tied to people actually serving in the military]. Also known as an [[encouragement design]].
