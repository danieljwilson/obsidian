### Re: Theory of Planned Behavior
[[behavioral intent|Behavioral intent]] in the [[theory of planned behavior]] model is a close proxy for [[motivation]]. I think that it makes sense to think that an equation like: 

$$intention = motivation + values + knowledge + affordances$$

Where motivation is fed into by [[first-order preference]] and where, for example, values could modulate motivation (e.g. tamp down [[first-order preference]] that you are trying to avoid)...

In this equation the norms from the [[theory of planned behavior]] would be captured by `values`. 

Perceived behavioral control seems closely linked to ideas of [[self-efficacy]].

Actual behavioral control (ability) seems closely linked to my idea of a combination of [[mindware]] and [[personality]]...e.g. do they have [[sustained inhibition]] ability to enact an intention, when required?
