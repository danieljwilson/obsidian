# Notes
Reflects the propensity to be self-controlled, responsible to others, hardworking, orderly, and rule abiding (Roberts, Jackson, Fayard, Edmonds, and Meints, 2009)

- Not a singular entity, but rather a family of dispositions such as:
	- industriousness
	- [[self control|self-control]]
	- orderliness
	- responsibility
	- conventionality
- Or in other words, a spectrum of constructs that describe individual differences in the propensity to be self-controlled, responsible to others, hardworking, orderly, and rule abiding (Roberts, Jackson, Fayard, Edmonds, & Meints, 2009).
- Subsumes current popular constructs such as [[grit]] and [[delay of gratification]] (Roberts et al., 2014)
- Predicts:
	- health and longevity
	- occupational success
	- marital stability
	- academic achievement
	- wealth^[This seems highly correlated with occupational success?]
- Important “non-cognitive” [[trait]] used in diverse fields such as economics, political science, and education
- In the [[Big Five]] taxonomy of personality traits
