# Notes
Says that a sample average can be brought as close as we like to the average in the population from which it is drawn simply by enlarging the sample.

**ALT**
The empirical probability will approach the true probability as the sample size increases.

- characterizes the behavior of sample averages in relation to sample size
- the _law of small numbers_ was introduced by the psychologists [[Daniel Kahneman]] and [[Amos Tversky]], who showed that people (even trained researchers) often behave as if the law of large numbers applies even to small samples, giving too much credence to results based on small datasets.
