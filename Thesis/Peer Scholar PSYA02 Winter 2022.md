# Notes
==**DEADLINE MARCH 17**==

 

### Phase 1: Create (2 of 19 points)  
-   2 POINTS: Drafts that are ==750 words or longe==r AND have ==at least three scholarly sources cited in the text and in a reference list== will receive the full 2 points for this section.
-   1 POINT: Drafts that are ==600-750 words== **OR** ==have fewer than three scholarly sources cited in the text and in a reference list== will receive 1 point for this section.
-   0 POINTS: Drafts that are ==under 600 words== **OR** are in ==bullet-point== form **OR** are ==missing both scholarly sources== will receive 0 points for this section. Drafts ==missing a reference list== **OR** ==missing in-text citations== will also receive 0 points for this section.

### Phase 2: Assess (4 of 19 points)
 - grade for this section will be based ==only on your constructive feedback==. 
 - You will receive **1 point for each peer evaluation** if your ==constructive feedback== is **specific** and if it **relates to the assignment description** found on Quercus and/or this rubric. Your feedback ==must be actionable== (in other words, it must ask the peer to DO something to change their work). You should be sure to describe specific ways that the peer could better address that description and improve their work.
 -  mechanical corrections (e.g., grammar, spelling, formatting) do not count!

### Phase 3: Revise and Reflect (13 points)
- 2 POINTS: **Reflection statement**
	-  you must ==describe in specific terms== how you chose to address the feedback that your peers gave you. For each piece of constructive feedback that you received, you should:
		- Describe how you incorporated that feedback into your paper, using specific examples from your work. **OR**
		- Explain why you chose not to incorporate that feedback into your paper, justifying your decision.
