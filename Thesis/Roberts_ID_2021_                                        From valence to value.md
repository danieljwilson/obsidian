# Notes
## Summary
Key function (hypothesized) of emotion is to provide information about [[stimuli]] and ==possible actions in the immediate environment==.^[I think of them as "gain" switches on a mixer...and turning them up and down can increase or decrease the likelihood of certain actions...can also have interaction between the different "knobs"...]

**Key Point**
>While most past decision-making research has investigated circumstances under which options are chosen despite associated unpleasant feelings, some research has shown that people often favor objects precisely because of the unpleasant feelings
they evoke.^[Is this not because there is a link between the negative emotion and some negative emotion? e.g. fear and excitement on a rollercoaster, sadness and catharsis listening to torch songs after a breakup, humiliation and sexual excitement in bdsm]

Affective valence may be an input in the decision process (affect-as-information theory^[this seems obvious?])

**Hypothesis**
Affective valence might be thought of as one source of evidence to the evidence accumulation process, weighted according to its contextual relevance.
- context determines weight
