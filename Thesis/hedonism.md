# Notes
The ancient hedonists (the Epicureans) thought that only a particular kind of pleasure—ataraxia, or  equanimity—constitutes well-being or flourishing (Inwood & Gerson, 1994).

- John Stuart Mill (1861/1979) argued that pleasures from engaging our uniquely human capacities are  more important, in virtue of their greater quality, to how well a person’s life goes.
