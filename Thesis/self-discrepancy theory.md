# Notes
States that individuals compare their ==actual== self to internalized standards or the ==ideal/ought self==.

>“a general theory relating different patterns of self-beliefs to different kinds of emotional-motivational predispositions” (Higgins, 1989, p.129)

- developed by Edward Tory Higgins in 1987
- idea is that internal disagreement causes emotional and psychological turmoil
- has links to self-inconsistency theory, the cognitive dissonance theory, and the imbalance theory


#### Elements
**Two standpoints**
1. Your own
2. Close other

**Three domains of self**
1. Actual
2. Ideal
3. Ought

**Six possible self-state representations**
1. **actual/own***
2. **actual/other***
3. ideal/own
4. ideal/other
5. ought/own
6. ought/other
