# Notes
The flexible allocation of [[attention]] in the service of [[goal directed behavior]] in the face of more [[habitual behavior]] or immediately compelling behaviors (e.g. [[first-order preference]]^[My example]).

- etymology: comes mainly from [[neuroscience]]
- also known as [[executive control|executive function]]
- used when describing the flexible use of low-level cognitive operations like [[attention]] and [[wprking memory]], whereas [[self-regulation]] is used when discussing the flexible adjustment of behavior in the real world
- some research finds it hard to distinguish [[cognitive control]] from [[general intelligence]] Conway et al. 2003, Engelhardt et al. 2016, Jewsbury et al. 2016).
