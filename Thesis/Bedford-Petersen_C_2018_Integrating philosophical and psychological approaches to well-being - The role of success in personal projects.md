## Summary
>[!Quote ]
>We believe most conceptions of well-being can agree that well-being involves success in one’s personal projects  and that personal projects should be a central construct for well-being assessments.

- Psychologists tend to reduce well-being to life satisfaction and the balance of positive and  negative  affect,  measured  through  self-report  (e.g.,  Diener,  Inglehart,  &  Tay,  2013;  Keyes, Shmotkin, & Ryff, 2002)

- Philosophical theories of well-being are often divided into three basic types:  [[hedonism]], objective theories, and desire satisfaction theories (Parfit, 1984)
