# Notes
Refers to the phenomenon where people make different decisions when they have personally experienced an event compared to when they are merely given a description of it.

- This concept has been studied extensively in the field of behavioral decision research, especially concerning how people make decisions under conditions of [[risk]] and [[uncertainty]].

**[[Decision from Description]]**: 
>In situations where people are provided with a description of possible outcomes and their probabilities (for example, a lottery with a 10% chance of winning), people tend to overweight rare events and underweight common events, a phenomenon known as probability weighting.

**[[Decision from Experience]]**: 
>In contrast, when people make decisions based on their personal experiences of the outcomes (for example, drawing from a deck of cards multiple times and observing the results), they tend to underweight rare events and make decisions that are closer to the expected value. This is because rare events might not have occurred in their experience, or they have happened infrequently, leading to an underestimation of their probability.
