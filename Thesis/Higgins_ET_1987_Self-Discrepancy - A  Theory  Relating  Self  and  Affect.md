# Notes
## GPT4 Summary
Higgins' theory identifies three self-domains:

1. **Actual Self**: This represents the attributes that someone (either oneself or another person) believes the individual actually possesses. It's the reality of who you are.
2. **Ideal Self**: This represents the attributes that someone hopes, wishes, or aspires to possess. It's who you ideally want to be.
3. **Ought Self**: This represents the attributes that someone believes he or she should or ought to possess. It's who you think you should be according to your duties and responsibilities.

These three selves can be further seen from one's own perspective or from the perspective of a significant other (e.g., a parent or partner), leading to six different self-state representations.

The theory states that discrepancies between these self-representations can lead to discomfort and specific kinds of emotional vulnerabilities:

- **Actual/Ideal Discrepancy**: If there's a gap between who you actually are and who you ideally want to be, it can lead to dejection-related emotions like disappointment, dissatisfaction, and sadness.
- **Actual/Ought Discrepancy**: If there's a difference between who you are and who you think you should be, it can lead to agitation-related emotions like anxiety, guilt, and restlessness.^[also shame and fear]

Higgins suggests that different individuals have different chronic orientations toward these self-guides, leading to patterns in their emotional experiences.

The theory has important implications for understanding self-regulation, motivation, and emotional experience, linking the cognitive representations of the self with affective responses.

## Thoughts
Seems to be dealing with the "observation" or "noticing" of one's intention behavior gap and the associated emotions that evokes. 

Not surprisingly the emotions are negative and correlate with "magnitude" of the gap and accessibility (i.e. attention paid to the gap).

Interesting distinction between ought and ideal "selves"

Doesn't look very much at individual differences
