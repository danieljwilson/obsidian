# Notes
In the context of decision-making and learning, a wicked environment refers to an environment that is **complex** and **dynamic**, where feedback can be delayed or misleading, making the relationship between actions and their outcomes hard to discern. 

- Decision-making is challenging in a wicked environment because of the high degree of uncertainty and ambiguity. 
- Wicked environments can make it hard for individuals or organizations to learn from their past actions and can lead to both under- and overconfidence in predictions and decision-making.
