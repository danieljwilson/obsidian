# Notes
- "everything is income" unless specifically excluded
- threshold
	- single $12,400
	- married filing separately $5

### How to avoid double taxation

==Foreign Tax Credit==
- reduces taxes dollar for dollar based on what you have already paid
- Form 1116
- usually eliminates tax bill

==Foreign Earned Income Exclusion==
- 108,700 for 2021 (earned income)
- Form 2555
- if you have lived abroad for more than 1 calendar year this generally makes you a "bonified resident" of the foreign country 

==Foreign Housing Exclusion==

==Child Tax Credit & Additional Child Tax Credit==

### Reporting requirements
==FBAR, FinCEN==
- Form/Report 114
- disclosure for $10,000 USD equivalent at ANY TIME across all accounts abroad
- doesn't matter for how short a time the money was there
- also applies to joint accounts

==FATCA==
- Thresholds start at $50,000 (in foreign accounts)

==Other==
- foreign business
- self-employment (totalization agreement can usually eliminate additional self-employment tax)
- capital gains (including home - there are some exclusions)
	- note for sale of home the first $250,000 of capital gains is tax free (also ways to eliminate gains above that...)
- cryptocurrency

### Deadlines
- June 15 if residing outside US
- October 15 is an extension you can file for (and can do by mail for December 15)

### Catching up on taxes
==Streamlined Procedure (SLP)==
- lets expats catch up without facing penalties
- can claim credits and exclusions
- file 3 years of past due tax returns (2018,19,20)
	- need to be filed before next return becomes delinquent (June 15 2022)
- file up to 6 past due FBARs
- requires absence from the US for 330 days during ONE of the THREE delinquent tax return years
- must file a non-willfulness statement (re not filing taxes)
	- form 14653
- need to **paper file**
