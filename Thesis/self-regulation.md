# Notes
The whole system of standards, thoughts, processes, and actions that guide people’s behavior toward **desired end states** ([Carver and Scheier, 2012](https://www.frontiersin.org/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2018.01231/full#B10)). These desired end states may be long-term goals, but can also refer to other standards or norms.

**ALT**
The dynamic process of determining a desired end state (i.e., a goal) and then taking action to move toward it while monitoring progress along the way (Carver & Scheier 1998)^[in the more recent definition they don't talk about it as goal setting...which seems included here]

**ALT**
The ability to regulate behavior in service of long-term goals.^[[[Enkavi_AZ_2019_Large-scale analysis of test–retest reliabilities of self-regulation measures]]]

- **umbrella term** that includes wide array of [[goals|goal]] relevant activities including:^[Fujita2011, Gollwitzer 1999, Ludwig et al. 2019, Shah et al. 2002, Wrosch et al. 2003)]
	- deciding which goal to pursue
	- planning how to pursue a selected goal
	- implementing goal attainment plans
	- shielding goals from competing concerns
	- sometimes abandoning goals
- can be divided into **emotional** and **behavioral** [[self-regulation]]
- subsumes the regulation not only of behavior but also of thoughts and emotions (e.g., Gross 2015)
- About reducing the frequency and intensity of strong impulses by managing stress-load and recovery.
- self-regulation is what makes self-control possible (or, in many cases, unnecessary)
- closely related to concept of [[self control|self-control]].
- [[cybernetic TOTE model]]^[[Powers, 1973](https://www.frontiersin.org/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2018.01231/full#B38)] of self-regulation identified 3 main components:
	- standards (aka desired end state)
	- monitoring (check for difference between current state/actions and desired end state)
	- operating (try to move yourself toward desired end state if there is a difference - closely aligned with [[self control|self-control]])
- **feedback loop**
- provides the entire scaffolding for successful [[goal setting]] and [[goal pursuit]].
- most research focuses on [[state|state level]] of the [[sociogenomic model]] (as opposed to [[trait|trait level]].
- sometimes referred to as the **noncognitive factor** important for success (Duckworth et al. 2019)^[what does it mean to say it is "noncognitive??"]
