# Notes
When people make decisions based on their personal experiences of the outcomes (for example, drawing from a deck of cards multiple times and observing the results), they tend to underweight rare events and make decisions that are closer to the expected value. 

This is because rare events might not have occurred in their experience, or they have happened infrequently, leading to an underestimation of their probability.
