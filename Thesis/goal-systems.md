[[goals]]
[[Arie W. Kruglanski]]

# Notes
The mental representations of motivational networks composed of inter-connected [[goals]] and means. Goals may be cognitively associated to their corresponding means of attainment and to alternative goals as well.

- Different goal-systems may be activated at the same time through environmental priming
- They may compete with each other for mental resources
- Means may be associated with other means
- **Facilitative** links may exist between vertically connected elements (i.e. goals and means)
- **Inhibitory** links may exist primarily between lateral elements (i.e. competing goals or competing means)

![[Pasted image 20210317110236.png]]

- Interconnections in these goal systems have two key aspects, their **form** and their **strength**.
- The number of means attached to a given goal may vary and so may the number of goals attached to a given mean
- The strength of any connection can vary as well
