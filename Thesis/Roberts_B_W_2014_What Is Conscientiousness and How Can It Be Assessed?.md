- correlations between traits arises because of direct causal relations among the traits^[This seems tricky. Could be many different reasons why one is punctual. #### Measuring Conscientiousness
Generally use [[self-report measure]]
- This can benefit from other methods (e.g. reports from knowledgeable observers)

Can also use [[implicit measures]]
- Have used [[Implicit Association Test]] to assess conscientiousness
- literature suggests ==implicit and explicit measures of conscientiousness are relatively uncorrelated==

Experimental or behavioral approaches can also be used
- can assess constructs from the conscientiousness "family"
- some research suggests 3 main domains:
	- [[impulse control|impulsivity]] - participant making choices between rewards that are delayed/immediate or probabilistic/certain
	- [[inattention]] - evaluate the participant’s ability to maintain alertness and receptivity for a particular set of stimuli or stimuli changes over time.
	- [[disinhibition]] - focus on ability to inhibit [[prepotent response]] or unwanted behavior.

## Thoughts
So conscientiousness is a subset of the category of "good" behavior. And then we are listing a bunch of traits that we think are associated with this particular region of category "good". 

What is the goal though? Just creating hierarchies of concepts/labels and trying to relate them? And then seeing which levels/concepts are most closely tied to outcomes of interest?

Also claims morals and virtue as additional constructs that are tied to conscientiousness...==what good traits are NOT tied to conscientiousness??==

In the end feels very much like a suitcase word^[credit to Marvin Minsky for the term]. So broad I am just not clear on what the value of defining it is? Would it make more sense to talk about acting responsibly? There is something about a sense of duty that seems common to many of the aspects - but it doesn't seem clear to me that self-regulation falls under the umbrella of conscientiousness - more that it is a trait/ability that facilitates dutiful behavior. 

I also think that many behaviors they are throwing in the conscientiousness suitcase could arise from other sources, such as ambition, which not only does not seem like the same thing as conscientiousness but in certain situations diametrically opposed.

I personally think of conscientiousness as tied with ethics/morality and there was no real discussion of that. But maybe that is mistaken. Just feels weird to think of a Nazi war criminal as a highly conscientious person though according to this definition they could be.

I think that I believe more in the **dynamical systems perspective** than the **psychobiological construct perspective**...what would it even mean for there to be a latent dimension of "conscientiousness" underlying all the traits. Even logically does not seem to hold up since many of the traits can be seen to be caused by things OTHER THAN conscientiousness. I think it makes more sense to think of conscientiousness as a high level concept/suitcase word that is just applied to things that seem similar based on [[Brent W. Roberts]] conception...feels a bit [[jingle jangle fallacies|jingle fallacy]]-ish.
