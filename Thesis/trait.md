# Notes
Relatively enduring constellations of thoughts, feelings, and behaviors that are elicited in diverse situations and that develop with the proper time and circumstances (Roberts, 2009).

**ALT**
Reflects the relatively enduring, automatic patterns of thoughts, feelings, and behaviors that differentiate people from one another and that are elicited in trait-evoking situations (Roberts, 2009; Roberts & Jackson, 2008).

**ALT 2**
Psychological (therefore organismic) structure underlying a  relatively  enduring behavioral disposition, i.e., a  tendency to  respond in  certain ways under certain circumstance (Tellegen, 1988)

ALT 3
A characteristic pattern of thinking, feeling,  or  behaving  that  tends  to  be  consistent  over   time and across relevant situations (Allport, 1961)

- traits are not reducible to [[behavior]]!^[apparently this is a claim made by numerous people (e.g. Bandura, 2012; Jackson, Hill, & Roberts, 2012)]
- traits are made up of **three** things: 
	- characteristic thoughts
	- feelings
	- behaviors
- Traits are not immutable (e.g. [[conscientiousness]])
- Changes are slow and incremental (Roberts & Mroczek, 2008).
- Believed to be **consistent**, albeit with systematic changes across the lifespan (Roberts et al., 2006).
	- e.g. individuals tend to become more [[conscientiousness|consciencious]] with age
- traits are separate from [[construct]]s and [[measures]]
- Key person in development = [[Gordon Allport]]
	- Suggested that there are:
		- ==Cardinal Traits==: rare, and dominate, usually developing later in life.
		- ==Central Traits==: general characteristics form basic personality foundations. While central traits are not as dominating as cardinal traits, they describe the major characteristics you might use to describe another person. Terms such as "intelligent," "honest," "shy," and "anxious" are considered central traits.
		- ==Secondary Traits==: sometimes related to attitudes or preferences. They often appear only in certain situations or under specific circumstances. Some examples include public speaking anxiety, or impatience while waiting in line.
