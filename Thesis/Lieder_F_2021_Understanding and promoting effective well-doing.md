## Chat GPT
The paper reviews the concept of well-doing, which refers to activities that promote well-being beyond individual self-interest, and explores how to promote effective well-doing. 

The paper highlights the need to develop a better understanding of the **mechanisms** and **outcomes** of well-doing, and suggests several emerging approaches that may be useful for promoting effective well-doing. 

These include enhancing empathy and compassion, promoting moral identity and values, fostering collaboration and social networks, and providing feedback and recognition for well-doing. 

The authors also emphasize the importance of addressing potential obstacles to well-doing, such as moral disengagement, perceived costs, and social norms that discourage well-doing. Overall, the paper provides a useful framework for understanding and promoting effective well-doing, and highlights the importance of further research in this area.

## Summary
Defines [[well-doing]] as the ==intentional pursuit of prosocial goals and values==.

**Main Point**
>some of the most socially-beneficial pursuits are often neglected because they are unintuitive^[I think you could expand this argument to any context...any goal you are trying to pursue likely has unintuitive paths/actions that would actually get you to your goal more quickly]

**Examples of well-doing**
- researching important problems
- advocating for good causes
- developing technologies that improve lives

**Requirements for well doing**
- prosocial values
- ability to make sound decisions

**Related Work**
>Maslow (1958) postulated that people have a fundamental psychological need to actualize their potential to effectively contribute to the greater good. According to Maslow, the frustration of this need causes suffering** once the more basic needs are sufficiently satisfied**.^[Important caveat]

**Attempted Implementations**
- interventions for fostering the development of purpose (e.g., Bronk, et al., 2019)
- orienting people towards socially and individually beneficial goals (e.g., Sheldon, Corcoran, & Prentice, 2019) and values (e.g., Lekes, et al., 2012; Kasser,2016)
- helping people gain wisdom (e.g., Grossmann,et al., 2019)

**Basic Research**
- moral learning (e.g., Cushman, Kumar,& Railton, 2017; Kohlberg, 1981; Eisenberg,Fabes, & Spinrad, 2007)
- character development (e.g.,Damon, Menon, & Bronk, 2003)
- meaning and purpose (e.g., King & Hicks, 2021; Bronk, 2013;McKnight & Kashdan, 2009)
- autonomous agency (e.g., Ryan & Deci, 2000; Sheldon, 2014)
- virtue (Fowers, Carroll, Leonhardt, &Crocklet, 2020)
- well-doing (e.g., Little, 2017;Sheldon, 2018)
