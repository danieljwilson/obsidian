# Notes
## Summary
Correlation implies association, but **not** causation.  
Conversely, causation implies association, but **not**  correlation.

- Association can exist in the absence of a causal relationship
- Correlation is a linear relationship
- Therefore can have association without correlation

![[Pasted image 20230815202751.png]]
- Association is the same as dependence and may be due to direct or  indirect causation.
- Correlation implies specific types of association such as monotone trends or clustering, but not causation
