# Notes
**Definition**: Multiple realizability refers to the philosophical concept that a particular mental state, function, or property can be instantiated by different physical states or processes. It is most often discussed in the philosophy of mind and cognitive science.

**Key Characteristics**:

- **Same Function, Different Physical States**: Multiple realizability emphasizes that the same mental function or property can be instantiated by different underlying physical or biological structures.
- **Challenges Reductionism**: The concept challenges the idea that there is a one-to-one correspondence between mental states and physical brain states.
- **Often Used in Philosophy and Cognitive Science**: Multiple realizability is central to debates about the nature of the mind and mental states, particularly in opposition to physicalism.

- Closely related to [[equifinality]]
