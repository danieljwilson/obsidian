# Notes

> Lab experiments offer control, field experiments offer realism, and digital field experiments combine control and realism at scale.

[[digital experiment]]s allow to increase the scale of the subject pool...
> Increasing the number of participants by a factor of 100 or more **is not just** a ==quantitative change==; it is a ==qualitative change==, because it enables researchers to learn different things from experiments (e.g., [[heterogeneity of treatment effects]]) and to run entirely different [[experimental design]]s (e.g.,large-group experiments)

- allows us to gather background [[pre-treatment information]] on subjects
	- enables more efficient experimental designs—such as [[blocking]] (Higgins, Sävje, and Sekhon 2016) and targeted recruitment of participants (Eckles, Kizilcec, and Bakshy 2016)—and more insightful analysis—such as estimation of [[heterogeneity of treatment effects]] (Athey and Imbens 2016a) and covariate adjustment for improved precision (Bloniarz et al. 2016).
- allow for much longer timescales [[always-on measurement]]
