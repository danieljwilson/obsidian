[[well being]]
[[objective well-being]]
[[personality]]

# Notes
Refers to the extent to which a person believes or feels that her life is going well. Generally seen to consist of [[affect]] and [[thoughts]]. 

==Affective component== captures people's feelings, emotions and moods - the hedonic level of affect
==Cognitive component==, also known as the contentment/structural component capture whether people's life expectations have been fulfilled (according to their cultural/societal standards)

- considered one of the best available proxies for a broader, more canonical form of [[well being]]^[Diener et al. 2018]
- Aristotle has expressed his interest on the topic claiming that human well-being, labeled as eudaimonia (εvδαιμoνια´ : Eu=Good, Daimon=spirit), is an activity of the soul expressing complete virtue
-  Diener defines happiness as people’s affective and cognitive evaluations of life
- comprises  both  the  absence  of  mental  disorder  and  the  presence  of  positive  psychological resources^[ [[Klug_HJP_2014_Linking Goal Progress and Subjective Well-Being - A Meta-analysis]] ]
- the genetically set-point theory of happiness promotes the idea that genetic influences set limits to the possible range  of  increases  in  SWB  (cf.  Lykken  and  Tellegen 1996).
