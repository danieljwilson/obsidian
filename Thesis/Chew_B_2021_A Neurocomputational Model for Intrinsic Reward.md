# Notes
## Summary
We don't have a good measure of human flourishing...
- GDP does not account for non market activities^[I would add that it does not account for its own activities either - GDP can rise during war, during the second war in the US "17 million new civilian jobs were created, industrial productivity increased by 96 percent, and corporate profits after taxes doubled"]

Claim to:
>  introduce a computational tool that measures the affective value of experiences (e.g., playing a musical instrument without errors^[this seems like complete bullshit]
>  
>  We go on to validate this tool with neural data, using fMRI to measure neural activity in male and female human subjects performing a reinforcement learning task that incorporated periodic ratings of subjective affective state.^[what is the point of fMRI?]

Claim:
> people overestimate both the intensities and durations of their hedonic responses to future events, and this is referred to as an impact bias

## Thoughts
- Okay so starts off with the earth-shattering revelation that economics does not capture the full remit of human values...
- Build a computational tool from theory that does not exist? What is your theory of human flourishing measurement that you can validate?
- Feels like a real [[motte-and-bailey]] argument...
- I mean if their main contribution is to confirm that we value non-market activity is that really moving the needle?
	- is it a discovery that people value intrinsic rewards?
	- is it a discovery that vmPFC activity is deferentially associated with value^[whatever value means...seems highly under-specified]?
- "confirming the utility of our approach for measuring the affective value of experiences and other non-market activities in individuals."
	- am I missing something? Didn't they just ask people about their affective state? Did anyone doubt the utility of this approach? Asking about affect as a measure of affect? 
	- and how can they confidently claim that their paradigm generalizes to "experiences" and "non-market activities"?
