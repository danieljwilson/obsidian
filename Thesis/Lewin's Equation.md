# Notes
The formula states that behavior is a function of the person and their environment.

**ALT**
States that an individual's behavior ($B$) is a function ($f$) of the the person ($P$), including their history, personality and motivation, and their environment ($E$), which includes both their physical and social surroundings.

$$B = f(P,E)$$
Where $B$ is behavior, $P$ is person and $E$ is environment.

- The equation was proposed as an attempt to unify the different branches of psychology (e.g. child psychology, animal psychology, psychopathology) with a flexible theory applicable to all distinct branches of psychology.
