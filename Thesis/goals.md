# Notes
The cognitive representation of a desired end state that a person is committed to attain.^[What do we mean when we say "committed" - definition from [[Inzlicht_M_2021_Integrating Models of Self-Regulation]]]

**SIMILAR**
"cognitive representation of possible states or outcomes that an individual seeks to attain" (Austin and Vancouver 1996; Elliot and Trash 2001)

**ALT**
Internal representations of desired states, where states are broadly construed as outcomes, events, or processes. 

**ALT2**
a current state of commitment to expend effort to affect one’s relationship with an end-state.^[[[Wilkowski_BM_2020_Lexical Derivation of the PINT Taxonomy of Goal - Prominence, Inclusiveness, Negativity Prevention, and Tradition]]]

- Internally represented desired states range from biological set points for internal processes (e.g., body temperature) to complex cognitive depictions of desired outcomes (e.g., career success)
- span from the moment to a life span and from the neurological to the interpersonal
- single goals cannot be understood when isolated from other goals and from the cognitive, behavioral, and affective responses organized in pursuing goals^[see [[Austin_J_T_1996_Goal Constructs in Psychology - Structure, Process and Content]]]
- goals are based on cognitive representations of the self, which are influenced by fundamental psychological needs as well as norms and rituals of culture (Cantor and Blanton 1996).

**There are Different types of goals.**
	- [[E. Tory Higgins]] suggests there are:
		- [[ought goals]]
		- [[chronic ideal goals]]
