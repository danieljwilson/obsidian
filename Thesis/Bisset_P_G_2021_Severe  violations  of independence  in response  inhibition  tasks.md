## Summary
Classic paradigm, used to understand:
- [[cognitive control]]
- [[response inhibition]]

Rests on theoretical foundation of [[race models]]

**Idea** 
1. Cognition and action can be directed toward [[goals]].
2. Goals can ==change==

[[stop signal task]] is a primary paradigm used to understand [[response inhibition]].

#### Assumptions^[that may not be true...]

>Context independence allows the model to use the observed go distribution on no-signal trials as an estimate of the distribution of go runners on stop-signal trials.

Also the [[race models|independent race model]] assumes [[stochastic independence]] which means that the finishing times of the go and stop processes are independent on a given trial.^[So even if you have seen the stop signal and respond incorrectly your timing should be the same as if you had not seen the stop signal at all...]

Found evidence that in some cases (very short SSD) this assumption is often violated as stop-failure RT is often longer than no-stop-signal RTs (this is not possible if they are independent).
