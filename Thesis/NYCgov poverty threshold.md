# Notes
Note...
[[National Academy of Sciences (NAS) Poverty Threshold]] and [[U.S. census bureau supplemental poverty measure (SPM)]] are not the same.

> The [[NYCgov poverty threshold]] has developed over time to align with that of the Supplemental Poverty Measure (SPM) released by the U.S. Census Bureau since 2011.

- The measure scales for household size^[Related to Davit Betson's scaling measure]


**MAIN DIFFERENCES**
==Geographic Adjustment==
- NYC Opportunity continues to use HUD’s Fair Market Rents^[representative of recently rented units of "standard" quality] for two-bedroom units. The FMR ratio for New York City differs from the ACS ratio (1.544225 vs. 1.34751) in 2018
- Because rent regulation is so widespread in New York City, rents at the median of the ACS distribution are not an accurate reflection of the market rate rental housing market.
==Adjustment for Poverty Unit Size==
- Use the three-parameter scale developed by David Betson
- Equivalence scale used to compare families of different sizes/compositions
