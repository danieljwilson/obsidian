## Summary
> We have each spent more than 50 years doing research that has had little impact. Even more lamentable is that our field, judgment and decision making (JDM), has on the whole had little impact during that span. We attribute that failure to the use of methodologies that emphasize testing models rather than looking for differences in behavior. The“cognitive revolution”led the field astray, toward the goal of studying model fit rather than comparing observable results. With modeling as the goal, experimentation was stultified. Simple tasks became dominant. Although a poor metaphor for real decision making, the gambling paradigm has lasted forever because the inputs to the decision are known to the researcher and thus easily modeled.

Seems that the general idea/critique is the classic problem of the hammer. We had some basic math tools that were fun and "clean" so we made the study of decision making fit those tools instead of really watching people make decisions...

	- [[Ward Edwards]] led the way...father of JDM research
- [[gambling paradigm]] as the "fruit fly" of research on risk.

> When behavior is complicated, a complex model may be needed to describe it,even if currently available technology cannot yet support a proper test. If modeling is paramount, though, the researcher will prefer to investigate simple, constrained behavior, so that an easily testable model can be employed. That's why the two-alternative gambling paradigm lives on after 70 years, even though it is a poor analog to almost any of the decisions people actually make. ==So much of what passes for progress consists of hair-splitting improvements in descriptive models of behaviors that occur only in laboratory settings.==

**IDEA**
Look at decision making from an **[[outcomes perspective]]**.

Focus on the two types of uncertainty:^[Knight, 1921]
1. uncertainty because consequences are probabilistic
2. uncertainty because not all consequences are known 

Study ==real decisions==.

Doesn't hold out much hope for [[naturalistic decision making]]...
