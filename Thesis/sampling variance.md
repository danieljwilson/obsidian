# Notes
related to descriptive [[variance]], but, unlike descriptive variance, sampling variance is also determined by [[sample size]].

$$V(\bar{Y}) = \frac{\sigma_Y^2}{n}$$

Where the numerator represents the variance of the underlying observations and the denominator captures the sample size (as it goes up, there will be less dispersion of repeated sample averages...)

- note that we often instead use the statistic of [[standard error]] which is just taking the square root of both sides of the equation...
