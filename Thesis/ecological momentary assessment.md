# Notes
Involves repeated sampling of subjects' current behaviors and experiences in real time, in subjects' natural environments

Four features:
1. Collection of data in real-world environments
2. Assessments that focus on individuals' current or very recent states or behaviors
3. Assessments that may be event-based, time-based, or randomly prompted
4. Completion of multiple assessments over time

- aims to minimize recall bias, maximize **ecological** validity, and allow study of microprocesses that influence behavior in real-world contexts.
- Tends to include physiological measurements, or asks questions relating to health, in addition to (or in lieu of) subjective measures.
- behavioral medicine field was the origin of EMA
- very similar to [[experience sampling|ESM]]
- happens in person's "natural" environment. [[experience sampling]] can take place in lab.
- open vs. closed survey questions
