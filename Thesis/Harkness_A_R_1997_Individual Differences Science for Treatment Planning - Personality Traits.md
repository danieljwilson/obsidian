# Notes
## Summary
==A plan is required BEFORE treatment begins due to various demands:==
- ethical
- legal
- financial

Personality trait assessment should be part of the treatment plan.

FOUR important gains for treatment plans that include individual differences in personality:
1. know where to target change efforts^[what are your personal gap mechanisms]
2. realistic expectations for change^[i.e. the IB gap itself]
	- idea of a **reaction range** - you can't turn a high neuroticism person into a low neuroticism person
3. match treatment to personality^[how is this different from 1?]
4. opportunities for development of (patient's) self-knowledge

**Their critique:**^[Idea is that it is the responsibility of the practitioner to stay on top of the latest scientific evidence....]
>Use  of favorite facts  in  neglect  of  the  total  evidence  requirement  characterizes  much  of  contemporary  treatment  planning.

**PERSONALITY TRAITS**
- Personality traits influence how people interpret life events.
- Traits exist in *individuals*, but traits lead to *population* concepts.
- They want to focus on three traits in particular that they see as related to treatment:
	1. [[extraversion]] (positive emotionality)
		- predisposition to enjoy life, to  become  engaged  in  its  activities,  to  seek  and  enjoy  the  company  of  others
	2. [[neuroticism]] (negative emotionality)
	3. constraint
		- extent to which they seek or avoid thrill and adventure,  are inhibited or uninhibited, and are traditional  or uninfluenced by imposed guidelines of the social order
- they see [[extraversion]] and [[neuroticism]] as [[orthogonal]], not opposite poles of a continuum.
- they believe all three have a substantial genetic component and have long term stability
- Across a large number of twin and adoption studies, the heritabilities of measures of most personality traits have ranged from  .30 to  .60 (G. Carey &  DiLalla, 1994),  with .50 being a commonly cited mean figure (Tellegen et al., 1988)^[test retest may not sometimes be all that much higher...]

- Many different characteristic adaptations can arise from any given set of "basic tendencies" ([[individual differences]])...

**ADAPTATION**
> Adaptation involves not only coping with and  creating **external** **circumstances** **but also adaptation to oneself**,  to  one's own  basic tendencies.
- also, the very modes of  adaptation selected are a function of those basic tendencies
- if a problem is just based on the environment it is simpler to "solve" than one that also involves one's basic tendencies^[example given of someone with a phobia of dogs based on a childhood incident...but not above average in fearfulness otherwise]

**Treatment focussed on adaptation change**
> A  major contribution of individual differences science to the  care  of human  problems  comes  in  focusing  interventions  on  characteristic adaptations rather than on basic tendencies. That  is, ==the main goal is not to change the  person== in  Vignette A  to  someone low in NE,  or to change the person in Vignette B  to  someone high in CN.  Rather, ==the main goal is to help patients  find more promising characteristic adaptations==. 
> 
> Another contribution of individual differences science is to remind the treatment  planner that  ==these new  characteristic adaptations  should  be constructed with sensitivity to the patient's basic tendencies==.^[this requires a high quality personality assessment]
