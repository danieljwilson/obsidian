# Notes
Refers to a cognitive process in which higher-level cognitive systems or processes exert influence over lower-level cognitive processes or behaviors.

- It involves the use of higher-level knowledge, expectations, goals, and conscious control to guide and regulate lower-level cognitive processes and behavior.
- characterized by the influence of pre-existing knowledge, beliefs, or intentions on how information is processed and behavior is performed
- involves the use of internal cognitive resources, such as attention, working memory, and executive functions, to direct and shape lower-level processes.
- often associated with deliberate, effortful processing, as it requires conscious awareness and active engagement
- plays a crucial role in various cognitive functions, including perception, attention, memory, problem-solving, and decision-making.
- allows individuals to use their internal cognitive resources to guide and regulate their cognitive processes in alignment with their goals and intentions.

**EXAMPLE**
> For example, when you engage in top-down control, you might consciously direct your attention to focus on specific aspects of a complex visual scene or use your working memory to hold information relevant to a particular task. This conscious guidance and regulation of cognitive processes help you filter out irrelevant information, make decisions, and carry out goal-directed behaviors.
