# Notes
>[!Quote]
>Behavioral intentions  are instructions that people give to themselves to behave in certain ways.^[Triandis, 1980, p. 203]
- assumed to capture the motivational factors that influence a behavior
- includes both **direction** (do vs not do $X$), and **intensity** (time/effort required)
- strength of intention an indication of how hard people are willing to try in order to perform a behavior
- behavioral intention can find expression in behavior **only if the behavior in question is under volitional control**
- closely related to [[goals]]

# Thoughts
I think that there can be specific and non-specific intentions, e.g. I intend to wake up at 6am vs. I intend to make the world a better place. The former have clear actions and success/failure metrics. The latter less so. Though you can operationalize them with concrete steps/actions.
