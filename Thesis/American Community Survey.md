# Notes
The **American Community Survey** (**ACS**) is a demographics survey program conducted by the US Census Bureau. 

- regularly gathers information previously contained only in the long form of the decennial census , such as ancestry, citizenship, educational attainment, income, language proficiency, migration, disability, employment, and housing characteristics.
- data are used by many public sector, private-sector, and not-for-profit stakeholders to allocate funding, track shifting demographics, plan for emergencies, and learn about local communities.
- Sent to approximately 295,000 addresses monthly (or 3.5 million per year), it is the largest household survey that the Census Bureau administers.
