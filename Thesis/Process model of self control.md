# Notes
Suggests that higher level goals can be viewed as higher order valuation systems that interact with lower order valuation systems.^[Sounds like [[first-order preference]] and [[second-order preference]]]

- Two strategies can be employed, either **proactive** or **reactive**
	- **Proactive** ==strategies attempt to avoid conflict before it emerges==^[Reminds me of trying to use structure to free up cognitive bandwidth]
		- Includes situation selection (don't buy cookies), situation modification (hide cookies you bought).
	- **Reactive** ==strategies are used to cope with existing temptations that conflict with important goals==.
		- Strategies include attentional deployment, cognitive response modulation
- impulse generation develops in an **iterative cycle** and so the earlier one intervenes, the more effective the corresponding strategies are
- Self-control failure tends to occur after initial self-control exertions because of shifts in motivation **away from control** and **toward impulses and gratification**. This shift in motivation consists of shifts in [[attention]] and shifts in emotional responding.
