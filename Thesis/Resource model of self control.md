# Notes
Makes two broad points about [[self control|self-control]]:
1. [[self control|Self-control]] is based on some central resource that powers all sorts of controlled behavior.
2. This central "[[self control|self-control]] resource" is limited, and depletes with usage

- primarily a model about time, where [[self control|self-control]] wanes over time, such that people exert less control at Time 2 if they have been continuously exerting control at Time 1
- has been largely discredited
- some think that is is just a form of mental fatigue and/or [[boredom]]
- might not add much to our knowledge
- suggests merely that control wanes with continued use, something anticipated by classic work on mental fatigue (Thorndike 1900)and the vigilance decrement (Mackworth 1948).
