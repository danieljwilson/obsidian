# Notes
System of simple feedback loops that contain four key elements:
1. a goal, set point, or standard
2. input about the current state of the organism with regards to the set point
3. a system that monitors for conflicts or discrepancies between the set point and the current state of the organism
4. a system that implements changes that reduce the discrepancy between current and desired states^[seems a lot like [[reinforcement learning]]]

## Thoughts
Note that this system doesn't say anything about where goals come from. Also there appears to be a step missing between **conflict monitoring** and **implementation**. This is where you would want to check for what **type of conflict** exists. 

Though perhaps you could assign this step (figuring out what type of conflict it is) to the conflict monitoring system. And you could include both the development of intentions (plans) and their implementation to the implementing system. Again, it gets muddy as it is collapsing intention and behavior.

In the conflict monitor stage it seems that there are two types of conflict that can occur:
1. errors of direction - i.e. you plan is not leading efficiently toward your goal
2. errors of execution - i.e. you are not successfully implementing your plans. In this case you have to decide if that is due to internal or external causes and in either case decide if there is another course of action that you should take. This could be changing plans (due to external conditions or self-knowledge...e.g. you just aren't a morning person and your plan involved getting up at 5am every day), or adding additional measures to make the implementation of your plans more likely (e.g. having a friend call you to wake you up in the morning).

==A simple example==
Say your goal is to run across a football field to get a cake at the other end.
Your goals is to have 0 yards between you and the cake and there is currently 100. This means you need to make a **plan**. This plan could be thought of as an [[implementation intention]] of sorts. It is the action or actions you believe that you need to take in order to achieve the goal and eventually align your **current state** with your **goal state**. You make a plan to walk to the cake. After a few seconds you reassess. You are closer to the cake but you realize that you are walking at a 45 degree angle to the direct path to the cake - you are making a literal error of direction. If you correct course you will get to the cake faster. After another few seconds you realize that someone else, behind you, is starting to run in the direction of the cake. Now your context/environment has changed. Even though your direction is now optimal if you don't change your execution they will overhaul you and you will not get the cake. You need to adapt your plan, and your intentions, in order to arrive at your goal. This means you have to run to (or trip the other person). 

This is a complex process. Understanding direction and execution all in the context of environment and adapting plans dynamically.
