## Summary
### Chat GPT
> Proposes a new taxonomy of goals that is based on four dimensions: Prominence, Inclusiveness, Negativity Prevention, and Tradition (PINT).

>The study used a lexical approach to derive the PINT dimensions, analyzing the frequency of goal-related words in multiple languages. The researchers identified a set of 60 goal-related words that were used across different languages and cultures, and then conducted a factor analysis to identify the underlying dimensions that these words represent.

>The results of the study showed that the PINT dimensions were consistently present across multiple languages and cultures, and that they were distinct from other goal-related dimensions, such as approach vs. avoidance goals and intrinsic vs. extrinsic goals.

>The PINT dimensions were defined as follows:
> - **Prominence**: the degree to which a goal is important or salient to an individual
> - **Inclusiveness**: the degree to which a goal encompasses other goals or values
> - **Negativity Prevention**: the degree to which a goal is focused on avoiding negative outcomes or maintaining the status quo
> - **Tradition**: the degree to which a goal is rooted in cultural or social norms and values

>The PINT taxonomy of goals provides a useful framework for understanding how individuals prioritize and pursue different types of goals, and could have implications for research on motivation, well-being, and cultural psychology.

**METHODS**
- A **lexical approach** and investigated the structure of goal-relevant words from the natural English lexicon.
- Through an intensive rating process, 1,060 goal-relevant English words were  located.
	- Interrater  agreement  was  relatively  high,  with  an  average  agree- ment between any two raters of 74%. This was due in large part to 4,707 words that were consensually evaluated as goal-irrelevant (out of 8549 words).^[This seems kind of wild - almost half of ALL nouns to rate were rated by at least one rater as goal-relevant??]
	- we conservatively eliminated only those words that were rejected by 75% of the raters or more (a criterion adopted before beginning the rating process).  7,315  were  eliminated,  leaving  1,234  words
- In Studies 1–2, two relatively large and diverse samples (total n = 1,026) rated their commitment to approaching or avoiding these goals. 
	- After  providing  informed consent, they received instructions asking them to indicate whether each of the 1,060 words (see Appendix A) represented a goal of theirs, using a 4 (I have an extremely strong commitment to this) to 0 (I have no commitment to this)to -4 (I have an extremely strong commitment to avoiding this)  scale.^[This seems like an INSANELY TEDIOUS task...]
	- instructed participants  to  only  indicate  something  is  a  goal  of  theirs  if  they  are currently committed to it (and not if they were only committed to it in the past), and if they are spending effort on it.^[This is going to miss any goals that we are having a hard time achieving - for people "currently" experiencing a gap between their intention and behavior you will MISS their goals...]
	- Took about an hour to complete which means they are rating 18 words a minute if they take no breaks which means about 3 seconds per word.^[Does not seem like a lot of time for thought, but is that needed? Seems like over the course of an hour would really deteriorate responses. Plus they were allowed to take breaks so likely less than 3 seconds per word]
- Used the items in the components discovered to create scales

**RESULTS**
- Principal component analyses yielded 4 replicable components: Prominence, Inclusiveness, Negativity Prevention, and Tradition (the PINT Taxonomy)
