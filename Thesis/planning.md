# Notes
Development  of specific alternative behavioral paths by which a goal can be attained (i.e., a strategy)

**ALT**
Tying higher level goals to  subgoals,  while stopping  short of  actually  engaging with  the  environment  (Anderson,  1993; Holyoak,  1990; Powers, 1973a).

- links goals  to  various behavioral  scripts,  tactics,  and alternatives
- facilitates  prioritization   decisions  among different  goals
- supports   the  revision  or  conversion  of  un- achieved goals in accordance  with higher level goals, or incom- ing information (i.e.,  feedback)
- planners  can  assess  the  likely consequences  of potential behaviors  based  on  simulations that  use  as input  past experiences and beliefs (or models) regarding the relationships among actions, person variables, and environments
- Repertoires of stored and accessible scripts (Lord & Kernan, 1987)  provide  goal  strivers  with  flexibility  and  alternatives.
