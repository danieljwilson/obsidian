# Notes
A goal-setting technique that involves visualizing a desired future outcome (positive fantasy), and then mentally contrasting this with the current reality, particularly the obstacles that stand in the way.

- stimulates a commitment to the goal and motivates individuals to take action to overcome these obstacles
- point of mental contrasting is to create a strong connection in your mind between the future (the goal) and the reality (the obstacles)
- can result in a boost of motivation to overcome the obstacles and achieve the goal
- psychological technique to create a sense of discrepancy that needs to be resolved, fueling the drive to take action
- most effective when used by individuals who are confident in their ability to achieve their goals
	- For individuals with low self-efficacy, it may be more effective to focus on building confidence and resources before engaging in mental contrasting.
- can be combined with [[implementation intention]]s ("if-then" planning) in a strategy called Mental Contrasting with Implementation Intentions (MCII) or "WOOP" (Wish, Outcome, Obstacle, Plan)
- developed by psychologist [[Gabriele Oettingen]]

==**Two Main Steps**==
1.  **Positive Fantasy:** This is where you allow yourself to dream and visualize the best possible outcome. You imagine reaching your goal in vivid detail, considering the feelings, sights, sounds, and other experiences associated with achieving this outcome.
2.  **Contrasting with Reality:** After you've created a vivid mental image of your desired outcome, you contrast it with the reality of your current situation. You think about the obstacles and challenges that stand between you and your goal. This can include personal weaknesses, external circumstances, or any other hindrances that may prevent you from reaching your goal.
