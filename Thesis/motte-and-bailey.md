# Notes
A form of argument where two positions that share similarities are conflated. One position tends to be controversial (the "bailey") and the other modest and much easier to defend (the "motte"). The arguer advances the controversial/strong position but then when challenged retreats to the modest uncontroversial position...the idea being that if the motte is not attacked then the bailey has not been refuted...
- informal fallacy
- named after the [motte-and-bailey castle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Motte-and-bailey_castle)
- Philosopher Nicholas Shackel, who coined the term prefers to speak of a motte-and-bailey _doctrine_ instead of a _fallacy_.
