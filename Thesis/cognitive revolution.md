# Notes
Using a computer metaphor, cognitivists argued that what went on inside, i.e., information processing, was critical.

- happened in late 1950s and 1960s
- Scientific psychology began to de-emphasize trying to control behavior, and instead began to construct models of mental processes
- this meant studying invisible processes ([[latent variables|latent construct]]s) via their outputs...
