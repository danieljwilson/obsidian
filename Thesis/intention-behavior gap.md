# Notes
Gap that occurs when the values (personal and cultural) or attitudes of an individual do not correlate to their actions.

- also known as [[value-action gap]] or [[intention-behavior gap|attitude-behavior gap]] or [[intention-behavior gap|belief-behavior gap]] or [[intention-behavior gap|knowledge-attitudes-practise gap]]
- most discussion of the phenomenon has taken place within environmental and social psychology
- [[goal intentions]] **do** predict [[behavior]], but not perfectly. A large meta analysis (Sheeran, 2002) found $r=0.53$...


Intentions can be
- internally motivated
- externally motivated


Gap **type** can be
- routine gaps (deviation from ideal routine)
	- minutes of exercise/day
	- habits can "bake in" a gap (or bake in its elimination)
- non-routine gaps
	- meant to buy a birthday card for a friend and forgot or ran out of time


Gap **importance** can be:
High -> Low
- Going to bed you realize that you had meant to wash the sheets

Gap **cause** can be:
- External
- Internal
	- Forgetting (esp. non-routine)
	- Unwilling (esp. conflict between first order and second order preference)
