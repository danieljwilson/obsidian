# Notes
## Summary

**Motivating Idea**
>Discussions about the replicability of psychological studies have primarily focused on improving research methods and practices, with less attention paid to the role of well-specified theories in facilitating the production^[Are we really "producing" empirical results? Seems a bit odd to put the researcher in the role of creator rather than observer...] of reliable empirical results.

**Goal**
1. Increase the quality of explanations^[Seems vague]
	- Later specify "mechanistic explanations"
2. Increase the precision of predictions
	- Two sources of imprecision:
		- often theories make predictions that cover a wide range of possible empirical outcomes...e.g. just suggests a non-zero correlation between constructs
		- many theories characterized by vaguely defined constructs

**Two elements**
1. Theory Specification
2. Theory Development

**Two approaches**
1. [[computational model|computational modeling]]
	- formalize psychological processes through mathematical models
2. [[construct validity|construct validation]]
	- focus on specific constructs and their operationalization
	- tests aspects of the theoretically specified operationalized construct and the hypothesized nomological network


- Problem stems from fact that few psychological phenomena are directly observable...so we theorize about [[latent psychological attributes|latent constructs]] (e.g. [[neuroticism]], [[working memory]], [[altruism]])
- Psychological theories often specify weak relationships (e.g., A decreases B) between poorly understood latent constructs (e.g., anxiety and inhibition).^[Is this the fault of the theory? Or simply a measurement problem? Or a construct specification problem?]
- Strong explanations give rise to precise predictions that can be empirically verified.^[This may be true but what about precise predictions without a strong explanation? Also what do they mean by precise? We seem to focus on significance above chance in many paradigms even if it is only slightly above chance...is this really "precise"?]
- relative to other sciences, psychology progresses slowly toward cumulative knowledge
- all scientific investigations are limited by the measures researchers use, and the measures used are similarly limited by the theoretical integrity underpinning them
- computational models offer a formal account of the parts of psychological processes and their functions, as well as a set of testable predictions^[Do we believe this? Are the predictions always testable? And it is not necessary that the model offers any mechanistic explanation...and isn't it risky to assume that if a good prediction is made then the mechanism is a match, given that there are infinite models that could explain the data?]
- Alongside the conceptualization of the focal construct, researchers also identify an entire universe of other constructs that are related to it (as causes, consequences, or correlates).^[This seems like a massive undertaking??] Also known as the [[nomological network]]
- The theoretical core of construct validation is the nomological network
- Construct validation defines the construct of interest (e.g., neuroticism) and positions it within a wider network of related constructs. In this approach the construct under investigation is of primary interest, whereas the specification of the relation-ships between latent constructs is secondary. In contrast, computational modeling focuses primarily on specifying the formal relationships through which constructs interact to solve a problem (e.g., how much cognitive resources to invest). In this approach the relationships between constructs are of primary interest, whereas the specification and measurement of the included constructs often come second.^[Really disagree with this claim and how they try and create this artificial difference. See thoughts for more] 
- Building a theory starts with a question.^[Again I disagree. I think that a theory starts with observation. These observations may lead to patterns that can then lead to questions/hypotheses...perhaps this is part of the problem. We just try and create a theory about what we are interested in?]
- Construct validation can help us better define our constructs, and computational modeling is a great tool for developing a precise understanding of the relationships between constructs.^[Fine, but also so what? This seems like the whole point of the paper...but a bit underwhelmed]
