# Notes
Represents the propensity to control impulses or, in the terminology of cognitive researchers, the ability to inhibit a [[prepotent response]].

**ALT**^[from [[Inzlicht_M_2021_Integrating Models of Self-Regulation]]]
The process of advancing one goal over a second goal when the two come into conflict.^[Seems like this is generally referring to trying to maintain a goal connected to [[second-order preference]] over a goal connected to [[first-order preference]]...]

- a form of [[self-regulation]]
- Often conceived of as a **conflict between short term and long term goals**...(e.g. [[delay discounting]] of goals with small immediate rewards vs. goals with larger yet delayed rewards...)^[Though in [[Inzlicht_M_2021_Integrating Models of Self-Regulation]] they don't limit it to this...can be conflicting immediate goals (I agree)]
- An executive function key for goal attainment.
- neural correlates = pre-frontal cortex
- delayed gratification, [[delay discounting]]
- On the negative end of the spectrum, one finds the tendency to be reckless, impulsive, and out of control.
- can be seen as a component of [[self-regulation]] (the "operate" component in the [[TOTE model]])
- some people^[Baumeister 2014] suggested that it requires [[effortful control]] (overriding unwanted thoughts, feelings and behaviors), however others suggest this is not a necessary component^[Galla & Duckworth 2015,Gillebaart & de Ridder 2015, Hennecke et al. 2019]
