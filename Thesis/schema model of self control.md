# Notes

Processes that cause [[ego depletion effect]]s occur around the activation of the fatigue/decreased vitality schema. This schema becomes activated via the registration of behavioral and physiological changes related to exercising self-control. The activation of the schema should instigate the motivation to conserve energy and, therefore, cause reduced effort and decreased performance in a subsequent self-control task. Moderator variables (e.g., energy supply) should influence the (non)activation of the fatigue/decreased vitality schema or its consequences.

- builds off of [[ego depletion effect]]
- crucial assumption is that people’s perceived self-control efforts are related to the experience of lowered subjective vitality
