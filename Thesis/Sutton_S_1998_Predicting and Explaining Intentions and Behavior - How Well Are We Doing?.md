# Notes
## Summary
Models from TPB and TRA explain:
- 40-50% of the variance in **intention**
- 19-38% of the variance in **behavior**

**EXPLANATION**
Identifying the determinants of intentions and behavior and specifying how these  factors combine.

**PREDICTION**
Does not care about the determinants per se...
- can be useful without explanation

==However a good explanatory model should allow for prediction!==

- According to author both [[Theory of Reasoned Action]] and [[theory of planned behavior]] can be seen as [[causality|causal]] models
- [[perceived behavioral control]] added on average 5% to the variance explained in intention (beyond attitude and subjective norm) and 1% to the variance explained behavior (beyond intention).
- TRA/TPB both assume there are not other factors that influence both intention and behavior^[seems like personality would fit this description? Specifically the elements of personality that are not captured by attitude and subjective norm or perceived behavioral control? Though what exactly would those personality constructs be??]

#### Why does TRA/TPB predict poorly?
- **Intentions may change**
	- longer the time gap the lower the correlation..
	- however, longer time gap can also provide more opportunity to do something (e.g. go to the dentist)
- **Violation of [[Principle of Compatibility]]**
	- predictor (intention) and criterion (behavior) should be measured at the same level of specificity (action, target, time context)^[potential drawback that the measures may be correlated partly because of shared method variance...i.e. person may answer questions in a similar way since they are worded in a similar way...]
- **Violation of Scale Correspondence**
	- e.g. "Consider  the following measures of intention  and behavior:  “I  intend to  engage in  vigorous  physical  activity at  least  15  times during the month of October” (measured on a 7-point scale ranging from  definitely to definitely not); “I engaged in vigorous physical activity - times  during the month of October.” Courneya (I 994) points out that the variation  obtained by these the two measures is not congruent"
- **Unequal number of response categories for Intention and Behavior**
- **Random measurement error**

---

**Other factors may also be involved not captured in TRA/TPB** (see Conner & Armitage, 1998, for a review).
- past behavior
- habit
- attitude toward the behavior
- self-identity
