# Notes
## GPT4 Summary
The authors propose that positive and negative emotions are not just simple, opposite reactions to pleasant or unpleasant stimuli. Instead, ==they are complex feedback mechanisms== that have evolved to serve specific functions in human behavior and thought processes.

In the control-process view, **[[negative affect]]** (such as sadness or anger) ==arises when there is a discrepancy between one's current state and one's goals or expectations==. This negative feedback prompts the individual to take action to reduce the discrepancy, effectively guiding behavior towards goal achievement.

Positive affect (such as happiness or contentment), on the other hand, is seen as a sign that one is on track to meet one's goals. This positive feedback reinforces the current behavior, signaling that the individual should continue on the current path.

Carver and Scheier emphasize that ==positive and negative affect are not just passive responses to the world== but are integral parts of a dynamic system that actively shapes how people pursue their goals and adapt to their environment. The **feedback loop of affect, behavior, and cognition** is seen as a fundamental aspect of human self-regulation.

Overall, this work advances a nuanced understanding of emotions, positioning them as essential components in the complex processes that govern human behavior and cognition, rather than simply reactions to external stimuli.
