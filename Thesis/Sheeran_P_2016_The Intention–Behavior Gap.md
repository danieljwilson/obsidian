# Notes
## Summary
Good opening quote:
>Bitter personal experience and meta-analysis converge on the conclusion that people do not always do the things that they intend to do.

[[goal intentions]] and [[behavoral intentions]]

- forming [[goal intentions]] and [[behavoral intentions]] crucial for securing long-term goals (Baumeister & Bargh, 2014; Kuhl & Quirin, 2011)
- in [[intervention]] [[goal intentions]] and [[behavoral intentions]] seen as key determinant of behavioral change
- [[goal intentions]] and [[behavoral intentions]] **predict behavior**

### Quality of Intention
#### Goal Dimensions
- **promotion** (vs. prevention; Higgins, 1997)
- **autonomy** (vs. control; Ryan and Deci 2000)
- **mastery** (vs. performance; Dweck and Leggett, 1988; Elliot & Church, 1997) 

Goals that focus on the **bold** side of the dialectic are ==more likely to be attained==

**Issues with goal setting**:
- Goals are often overly optimistic...(see [[planning fallacy]])
	- This can also be a self-control strategy that helps people to deal with obstacles during goal pursuit.
	- people allocate more effort to pursuit of optimistic goals than more realistic goals
	- optimistic goals can contribute to the [[intention-behavior gap]], but can also lead to higher levels of absolute performance
- [[cold-to-hot empathy gap]]

- concrete or specific goals (e.g., “gain an A grade”) engender better performance than general or ‘do your best’ goals (for a review, see Locke & Latham, 2013.

Idea that we are often poor at [[goal setting]].
- overly optimistic (Buehler, Griffin, & Ross, 1994)
- setting goals in "cold" state
- [[optimism bias]] which can actually lead to better performance at the same time as a bigger [[intention-behavior gap]]

SES moderates [[intention-behavior gap]] (Conner, McEachan, et al., 2013)
- [[self-efficacy]] and perceived behavioral control do not, consistently

#### Basis of Intention
- Internally generated intentions more predictive of behavior than externally motivated intentions (social pressure of norms)
- Many intentions present a conflict between what people **want** to do and what they feel they **should** do (Milkman, Rogers, & Bazerman, 2008)
	- Reminds me of [[first-order preference]] and [[second-order preference]]
- [[self-schema|Self-schema]], or identity associated with gap 
	- e.g. is the activity connected to my self concept?

#### Properties of intention
- Direction
- Intensity
- Accessibility
- Stability (perhaps the strongest predictor of behavior)

#### Intention-Behavior blocks
- **Getting started**
	- Can just **forget**
	- Failure to prepare
	- Goal revision (perhaps related to [[planning fallacy]] or [[cold-to-hot empathy gap]])
- **Keeping ongoing goal pursuit on track**
	- [[the ostrich problem]]: motivated avoidance of progress monitoring
	- derailed by competing goals
	- bad habits
	- disruptive thoughts/feelings
	- distractions
	- temptations ([[first-order preference]])
	- low [[willpower]] (personality factors)
		- related to low levels of executive function, low conscientiousness, low future time orientation, low volitional control, low mindfulness
- **Bringing goal pursuit to successful close**
	- withdrawing effort before completion (seems tied to the previous cause??)
	- continued engagement in futile course of action
	- over-extending yourself

#### Intention-Behavior Tools
- **If-then plans**
	- [[implementation intention]]
	- involves identifying relevant ==opportunities== and ==obstacles== and figuring out responses to each
	- helps disengage from futile goal striving
	- endow responses with features of automaticity^[I am on board with this - creating systems...reducing in the moment decision making]
- **Progress monitoring interventions**
	- discrepancies from standards signal need for [[self-regulation]]
