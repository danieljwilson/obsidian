# Notes
A theory of [[intention-behavior gap|attitude-behavior consistency]] postulating that the process by which attitudes influence behavior **differs** ==according to the amount of deliberation involved==.

**Version 1**
When people are **motivated** and **able to deliberate** about their actions, attitudes influence behavior in a manner similar to that postulated by the [[theory of planned behavior|theory of reasoned action]].

**Version 2**
When people are **not motivated** or **able to deliberate** about their actions, attitudes toward the target of the behavior can be activated in [[memory]] and affect the way the target is perceived.
