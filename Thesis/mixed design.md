# Notes
Researcher compares the change in outcomes for people in the treatment and control groups.

- combines the improved precision of within-subjects designs and the protection against confounding of between-subjects designs
- when researchers already have [[pre-treatment information]], as is the case in many [[digital experiment]]s, mixed designs are generally preferable to between-subjects designs because they result in improved precision of estimates.
