# Notes
## Summary
### Chat GPT
Proposes a new personality trait called grit, which is defined as perseverance and passion for long-term goals. The authors argue that grit is distinct from other personality traits, such as conscientiousness and extraversion, and is a better predictor of success in a variety of contexts, including education, sports, and the military. The paper presents several studies that provide evidence for the validity and reliability of the grit scale, which measures the degree of grittiness in individuals. The authors also discuss the potential mechanisms underlying grit, such as the role of intrinsic motivation and the growth mindset. Finally, the paper highlights some practical implications of grit, such as the importance of cultivating grit in individuals to promote achievement and well-being.

**Conscientiousness**
>Uncorrected correlations between conscientiousness and job performance ranged from r .09 to r .13, depending on the occupational group. In a meta-analysis of confirmatory studies of personality measures as predictors of job performance, Tett, Jackson, and Rothstein (1991) observed a sample-weighted mean correlation between conscientiousness and job performance of r .12.

**RESULTS**
- Did not correlate with IQ
- related to Conscientiousness (r .77, p .001) 
 - Neuroticism (r .38, p .001), 
 - Agreeableness (r .24, p .001)
 - Extraversion (r .22, p .001)
 - Openness to Experience (r .14, p .001)
>In a binary logistic regression predicting high versus low career change from grit, age, and all Big Five traits, grit was the only significant predictor (OR 0.65,  .44, p .001). Individuals who were a standard deviation higher in grit than average were 35% less likely to be frequent career changers.

Point out that social desirability bias could be a problem as the questions are fairly transparent.

## Thoughts
Nicely laid out intro. Not sure what the takeaway is practically speaking. Conceptually it is not that crazy to say that working hard and sticking to your goals has value.

The least generous interpretation is that they found a series of questions that seem thematically linked and that are related to real world performance outcomes...

Useful for screening people? Hiring, school, etc.?
Useful for providing focused support? Tailoring interventions?
