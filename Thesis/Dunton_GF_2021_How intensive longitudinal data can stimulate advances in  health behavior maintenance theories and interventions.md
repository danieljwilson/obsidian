# Notes
## Summary
With intensive longitudinal     data     (ILD)      methods,   new   theories   of   health   behavior    maintenance can be developed and tested that  define  **progression**,  **stagnation**,  and  **regression**   along  the  behavior  change  continuum  based   on the nature of the interplay between reactive  and reflective systems that guide behavior.

Theories of health behavior maintenance tend to address **reactive** (i.e., automatic) or **reflective** (i.e.,  deliberative) decision-making processes, but rarely both.
