# Notes
## Summary
>[!Main idea]
>we test a  model of [[conscientiousness]] as operating via goal setting systems specified by Goal Setting Theory

#### Study 1
>[! Important]
>measures  of ten goal setting motives proposed in GST were developed and tested. All scales showed  statistically significant and strong associations with C (mean β = 0.46)

#### Study 2
>[! Important]
>Pre-registered  and improved  scale reliability and coverage (adding two goal setting systems omitted in Study 1). All predicted associations  replicated with similar large magnitudes. Structural modelling estimated the latent association at 0.89

> Conscientiousness (C) is manifested in behaviors of orderliness, responsibility, industry, ambition, dutifulness and rule-abiding ^[Not sure this is a sensible way to think of things...are we not just giving a range of behaviors that we "like" as emanating from this singular construct/trait? Is this meaningful?]

**The "real world" implications**
>powerfully  predicts a host  of  desirable  outcomes  including  education  and  work  (Richardson,  Abraham,  &  Bond,  2012;  Wilmot  &  Ones,  2019)  and  extending  to  longevity  and lawfulness (Moffitt  et al.,  2011). ^[Check up on these references...how "powerful" are these predictions actually??]

**The problem they purport to address**
> A widely  recognised  problem  for  personality  science  is  that  traits  such  as  C  are  pre-  theoretical: enumerating the correlates, but not identifying neuropsychological mechanisms of that trait (Inzlicht, Werner, Briskin, & Rob- erts,  2021).

==Yes it feels like we are just grouping a set of behaviors together that we deem "good" and similar to each other in one conceptual dimension. This conceptual dimension has no "representation" in the brain that we know of...or even really have reason to believe exists...==

> There  is  thus  a  need  to  understand  the  cognitive/  motivational mechanisms and pathways by which C causes the wide  range of life outcomes observed.

==What does this have to do with "C"? If we understand a mechanism that "causes" an important life outcome what does that look like? Identifying a certain connectivity pattern in the brain? Just finding a series of questions that closely correlate with an outcome of interest that we claim are closely related to a unified concept that we can point to?==

The gap is REAL. Focus on the gap. That is why implementation intentions are actually useful.

> This work would link individual differences research on C to multiple active areas of research on brain areas  linked to motivation and effort (e.g., Shenhav et al., 2017)

==This seems like a total crock.==




#### Conclusion
>[!Quote ]
>This tight  mapping of conscientiousness  to systems uncovered by GST corroborates  a model of C as strongly rooted  in  intentional goal setting, potentially explaining why C is linked to attainment across such diverse swathes of life  by progressing  any pathway impacted by goal setting. Framing C in terms  of effects on goal setting systems  integrates personality research with motivation research and suggests targets for managing performance.
