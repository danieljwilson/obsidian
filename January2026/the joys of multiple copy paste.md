About 6 years ago my friend [Hause](https://www.hauselin.com) told me about his deep and abiding love of [Alfred](https://www.alfredapp.com).

I am definitely not the power user of it that he is but one single feature[^1] has made it COMPLETELY worth the onetime price I paid: Alfred’s clipboard history.

Copy pasting is one of those inevitable minor daily frictions 
Instead of only pasting the _last_ thing I copied, Alfred keeps a rolling history of everything I copy throughout the day. With a simple shortcut, I can open a searchable list of recent clipboard items and paste exactly what I need — text, links, code snippets, or images — even if I copied them hours ago.

Why this matters:

• No more re-copying or flipping between tabs  
• Faster writing and editing (especially when assembling notes, emails, or code)  
• Reduced cognitive load — I don’t have to “plan” my copies  
• Works like a lightweight, temporary knowledge buffer throughout the day

In practice, it feels like having an extended working memory for my computer. Small friction disappears, and context switching drops noticeably.

If you want similar functionality, there are several options:

• Alfred (Powerpack) — clipboard history + search + automation (what I use)  
• Raycast — modern alternative with built-in clipboard history  
• Paste or Pastebot — dedicated clipboard managers with visual history  
• Windows — Win+V clipboard history

It’s a tiny upgrade, but one of those tools that quietly compounds productivity every day.

[^1]: Kind of like the find my phone feature on my Apple watch
