### [[Build AI Products Locally]]: 10 Hours of Free Workshops with a Google DeepMind Researcher
> Each workshop is self-contained, so you can start wherever you want. We’ve linked to YouTube timestamps and notebooks throughout so you can jump straight to what interests you. Here are the five workshops:

1. **[Building AI Apps for Real-World Use Cases](https://hugobowne.substack.com/i/191101929/building-ai-apps-for-real-world-use-cases-from-basics-to-production)**[:](https://hugobowne.substack.com/i/191101929/building-ai-apps-for-real-world-use-cases-from-basics-to-production) Build your first local AI app end-to-end: model, data, front-end, database, evaluation, and optimization;
    
2. **[Building AI Agents with Gemma 3](https://hugobowne.substack.com/i/191101929/building-ai-agents-with-gemma-3)**[:](https://hugobowne.substack.com/i/191101929/building-ai-agents-with-gemma-3) Build an AI agent from scratch, from manual tool calling to discoverable tools with MCP;
    
3. **[From Images to Agents -Building and Evaluating Multimodal AI Workflows:](https://hugobowne.substack.com/i/191101929/from-images-to-agents-building-and-evaluating-multimodal-ai-workflows)** [](https://hugobowne.substack.com/i/191101929/from-images-to-agents-building-and-evaluating-multimodal-ai-workflows)Teach an LLM to see and act on images, then wire it into an agent;
    
4. **[Fine-Tuning Open-Weight Language Models](https://hugobowne.substack.com/i/191101929/fine-tuning-open-weight-models-a-hands-on-deep-learning-primer)**[:](https://hugobowne.substack.com/i/191101929/fine-tuning-open-weight-models-a-hands-on-deep-learning-primer) Train a model to do what you want: classification, style transfer, and learning the deep learning essentials along the way (you’ll also build an eval harness for your fine-tuned models!);
    
5. **[Building Agents and Eval Harnesses with Local LLMs:](https://hugobowne.substack.com/i/191101929/building-agents-and-eval-harnesses-with-local-llms-with-ravin-kumar-google-deepmind)** Build an agent that runs on a phone and learn how to rigorously evaluate agentic behavior.


### Questions to Answer
- You understand gradient descent intuitively. You can explain what a loss function does and why matrix multiplication matters for neural networks.
- You can build, train, and evaluate a classification model on a real dataset.
- You can build and train a neural network in PyTorch. You understand Transformers well enough to explain self-attention to a non-technical person.
- You have built a RAG application that answers questions from your own documents. You have deployed at least one LLM-powered app.
